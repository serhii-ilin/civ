# MOD-HMH: Health and Mental Health

## Purpose

Design health, public health, and mental health as civic infrastructure.

## Design Requirements

- Universal access to preventive, acute, chronic, and mental healthcare.
- Community-level early warning and support systems.
- Anti-isolation architecture across physical, social, and digital environments.
- Trauma-informed institutional default behaviors.
- Destigmatization mechanisms built into culture, education, and language.
- Professional mental health access as a universal right.
- Public health readiness for pandemics, addiction, disability, aging, and chronic disease.
- Safeguards against coercive or surveillance-heavy health interventions.

## Core Questions

- RQ-HMH-001: What health services are guaranteed at each scale?
- RQ-HMH-002: How can early warning systems protect people without becoming surveillance?
- RQ-HMH-003: Which built-environment choices reduce loneliness, stress, and preventable disease?
- RQ-HMH-004: How are family systems, trauma, addiction, and disability supported?
- RQ-HMH-005: What public health powers exist during crisis, and what limits apply?
