# MOD-GOV: Governance

## Purpose

Design a capture-resistant, legitimate, competence-aware governance system with self-correction mechanisms.

## Design Requirements

- No single point of power failure.
- Distributed, multi-branch, multi-scale authority.
- Local cell, regional federation, and national or global tier.
- Sortition plus expertise hybrid for civic participation and technical review.
- Competence-gated leadership with transparent, non-gameable assessment where possible.
- Rotation, audit, appeal, removal, and term limits for authority-bearing roles.
- Radical transparency for institutions, balanced against individual privacy and security.
- Member-facing observability for public decisions, budgets, enforcement actions, institutional performance, social-process health, and unresolved risks.
- Permanent adversarial review body with authority to inspect and challenge governance decisions.
- Constitutional self-correction mechanisms that avoid both paralysis and revolutionary rupture.

## Core Questions

- RQ-GOV-001: Which powers must be local, regional, or central?
- RQ-GOV-002: How are competence, ethics, and emotional fitness assessed without creating credential capture?
- RQ-GOV-003: How can sortition, expertise, and democratic legitimacy be combined?
- RQ-GOV-004: What anti-corruption mechanisms work structurally rather than symbolically?
- RQ-GOV-005: How can dissent and minority rights be protected during crisis?
- RQ-GOV-006: What government and social-process data must be visible to members, and what must remain private or security-restricted?
