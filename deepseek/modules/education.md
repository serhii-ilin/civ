# MOD-EDU: Education

## Purpose

Design an education system that produces resilient, capable, ethical, emotionally intelligent, practically skilled, and civically competent people.

## Design Requirements

- Early childhood through adult lifelong learning.
- Critical thinking, scientific literacy, statistical reasoning, civic literacy, and media literacy.
- Emotional intelligence, conflict resolution, relational health, and trauma awareness.
- Practical self-sufficiency skills: food, repair, finance, health, digital systems, emergency readiness, and local governance.
- Intrinsic motivation, autonomy, mastery, and purpose.
- Assessment that supports learning and capability rather than credential sorting alone.
- Reskilling systems for technological and economic transition.

## Core Questions

- RQ-EDU-001: What capabilities should every adult reliably have?
- RQ-EDU-002: How are teachers selected, supported, and evaluated?
- RQ-EDU-003: What should be standardized nationally, and what should be locally adapted?
- RQ-EDU-004: How can education support pluralism without abandoning shared civic competence?
- RQ-EDU-005: How does the system prevent credentialism from becoming class reproduction?
