# MOD-ECO: Economy

## Purpose

Design an economic system that secures basic needs, rewards contribution, prevents extreme concentration, supports innovation, and remains within ecological limits.

## Design Requirements

- Decouple survival security from labor coercion.
- Incentivize contribution without mandating it.
- Treat ecological cost as a real economic cost through natural capital accounting or comparable mechanisms.
- Define a tax philosophy consistent with the value constitution.
- Prevent extreme wealth and power concentration by design.
- Support innovation and entrepreneurship while preventing monopolization.
- Include a commons layer for essential infrastructure: water, energy, data, education, healthcare, and core mobility.
- Recognize care work, household labor, and community maintenance as economically real.

## Core Questions

- RQ-ECO-001: Which basic goods and services belong in the commons layer?
- RQ-ECO-002: What mix of markets, cooperatives, public provisioning, and commons best fits the value constitution?
- RQ-ECO-003: How should land, natural resources, automation rents, and monopoly rents be taxed or governed?
- RQ-ECO-004: How does the system prevent poverty traps, rent extraction, and inherited oligarchy?
- RQ-ECO-005: What incentives support high contribution without coercive precarity?
