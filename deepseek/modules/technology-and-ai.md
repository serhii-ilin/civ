# MOD-TAI: Technology and AI

## Purpose

Define technology governance, AI use, data rights, cybersecurity, digital infrastructure, and safeguards against technological concentration or abuse.

## Design Requirements

- Data rights, privacy, consent, minimization, portability, and deletion.
- Algorithmic accountability for public-sector and high-impact private systems.
- AI-assisted governance limits, auditability, and human accountability.
- Cybersecurity standards for civic infrastructure.
- Digital identity with strong privacy and anti-exclusion safeguards.
- Secure observability infrastructure for institutional logs, public metrics, audit trails, anomaly detection, and member access controls.
- Automation transition policy for labor, education, ownership, and taxation.
- Biosecurity and dual-use technology governance.
- Open standards and interoperability for essential digital infrastructure.

## Core Questions

- RQ-TAI-001: Which decisions may AI advise, and which decisions may it never make?
- RQ-TAI-002: How are algorithms audited, appealed, and retired?
- RQ-TAI-003: Who owns data generated by people, institutions, sensors, and public infrastructure?
- RQ-TAI-004: How does society distribute automation gains?
- RQ-TAI-005: What cyber and biosecurity risks could break the framework?
- RQ-TAI-006: What technical architecture provides high observability for members while enforcing privacy and external security boundaries?
