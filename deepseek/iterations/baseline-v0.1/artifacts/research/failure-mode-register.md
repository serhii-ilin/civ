# ART-006: Failure Mode Register

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`
Status: draft

## Purpose

Catalog the most dangerous failure modes for societies, their historical examples, causal mechanisms, and potential mitigations. This register feeds directly into simulation stress-testing and architectural safeguards.

## Failure Modes

| Risk ID | Failure Mode | Historical Examples | Mechanism | Severity (1-10) | Mitigations | Residual Risk | Test in Simulation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| RSK-001 | Elite Capture | Late Roman Republic, post-Soviet oligarchy, modern regulatory capture | Wealthy/powerful actors gradually rewrite rules to benefit themselves: campaign finance capture, regulatory capture, revolving doors, dynastic wealth concentration, media consolidation. | 9 | Public campaign finance; strict revolving-door periods; anti-monopoly enforcement; progressive wealth taxation; citizen assemblies for redistributive decisions; independent anti-corruption body with prosecution power; wealth-concentration metrics trigger automatic redistributive mechanisms. | Medium: capture is adaptive; will find new routes | Yes: simulate elite-agent strategies to concentrate power over N generations |
| RSK-002 | Institutional Entropy / Democratic Backsliding | Weimar Germany, Hungary post-2010, Venezuela, Turkey, Russia | Democratic institutions hollowed out through legalistic rather than violent means: court-packing, media capture, electoral manipulation, civil-society repression, emergency-power abuse without expiration. | 9 | Constitutional eternity clauses for core rights; multi-branch appointment of judges; independent electoral commission with technical membership; mandatory emergency-power sunset; civil-society protection funding insulated from government budget; international democratic-alliance membership with mutual defense clauses. | Medium-High: formal rules can be subverted by determined actors | Yes: simulate gradual institutional degradation under concerted elite pressure |
| RSK-003 | Ecological Overshoot | Easter Island, Mayan Classic collapse, Aral Sea, global climate change | Resource consumption exceeds regenerative capacity: deforestation, soil depletion, aquifer exhaustion, fisheries collapse, climate destabilization, biodiversity loss. | 10 | Ecological boundaries as legal caps with automatic triggers; natural-capital accounting integrated into all economic decisions; circular-economy mandate; ecosystem-service payments; independent ecological guardian body with veto over boundary-exceeding projects; ecological-footprint per-capita caps. | High: global commons problem; some boundaries already exceeded globally | Yes: simulate resource-depletion scenarios at local and system scale |
| RSK-004 | Corruption Normalization | Zaire under Mobutu, post-Soviet transition states, systemic graft | Corruption transitions from deviant behavior to expected norm: petty bribery, grand corruption, state capture, kleptocracy. Trust collapses, competence incentives invert, public goods degrade. | 8 | Structural anti-corruption: asset declarations with verification, independent prosecutors with guaranteed tenure, procurement transparency with open bidding, whistleblower protections and rewards, unexplained-wealth orders, civil-service pay competitive enough to reduce bribery incentive, corruption-conviction metrics public and real-time. | Medium: requires constant vigilance | Yes: simulate corruption spread through agent networks under varying institutional constraints |
| RSK-005 | Value Exhaustion / Ideological Collapse | Soviet Union late 1980s, many millenarian movements | Governing ideology loses credibility, citizens withdraw loyalty, institutional compliance collapses, parallel power structures emerge, state becomes hollow shell. | 7 | Pluralistic values rather than monolithic ideology; institutional legitimacy maintained through demonstrated competence and fairness; multiple sources of meaning (civic, cultural, spiritual, scientific) not dependent on single creed; orientation toward continuous self-correction rather than doctrinal purity. | Medium: no society is immune to value crises | Yes: simulate loss of legitimacy contagion through agent population |
| RSK-006 | Bureaucratic Overgrowth / Complexity Collapse | Late Western Roman Empire, some analysis of modern regulatory states | Increasing complexity of administration, regulation, and compliance produces diminishing returns; systems become too complex for anyone to understand or reform; sclerosis and collapse follow. | 7 | Sunset clauses on all regulations; regulatory budget (remove one rule to add one); subsidiarity: push decisions to lowest effective level; periodic simplification commissions with binding authority; mandatory regulatory-impact assessments with complexity metric; digital simplification and automation of compliance. | Medium-High: complexity is self-reinforcing | Yes: simulate bureaucratic-cost growth under varying design parameters |
| RSK-007 | Generational Drift / Founding-Energy Dissipation | Most intentional communities after 2-3 generations, post-revolutionary states | Founding generation's commitment, shared experience, and ideological clarity weakens as succeeding generations inherit institutions without the formative struggle that created them. | 7 | Civic education that transmits founding rationale without indoctrination; institutional rituals that renew commitment; youth representation in governance from early age; deliberate renewal mechanisms (constitutional conventions, citizen assemblies, periodic re-founding moments); avoid personality-cult leadership that cannot transfer. | Medium-High: appears universal in non-coercive systems | Yes: simulate transmission of civic commitment across agent generations |
| RSK-008 | External Conquest / Military Collapse | Carthage, Aztec Empire, Poland-Lithuania partition | Military defeat by external power: conventional invasion, nuclear threat, coup d'etat, or economic strangulation. | 9 | Multi-layered defense: deterrence, defense-in-depth, civil-defense integration, economic self-sufficiency floor, diplomatic alliance network, cybersecurity, information-warfare defense, supply-chain security, strategic reserves. | Medium: no defense is absolute | Yes: simulate external-attack scenarios across domains (military, cyber, economic, informational) |
| RSK-009 | Pandemic / Biological Catastrophe | Plague of Justinian, Black Death, 1918 influenza, COVID-19, potential engineered pathogens | Pathogen outbreak overwhelms health system, disrupts economic and social function, degrades institutional trust through mismanagement. | 8 | Universal healthcare with surge capacity; early-warning pathogen surveillance (wastewater, genomic, syndromic); strategic medical-supply reserves; pandemic-response protocols with civil-liberties safeguards; biosecurity governance for synthetic biology; global health cooperation. | Medium-High: zoonotic spillover and bioengineering risks increasing | Yes: simulate pandemic scenarios with pathogen characteristics and institutional response delays |
| RSK-010 | AI / Technological Disruption and Concentration | Industrial Revolution disruptions (Luddites, labor unrest), current AI deployment debates | AI automation displaces labor faster than transition systems absorb; AI control concentrates in few hands; algorithmic governance replaces human accountability; autonomous weapons or cyber weapons proliferate; AI-generated misinformation degrades shared reality. | 8 | Automation transition policy: reskilling, job guarantees, reduced work hours, automation-dividend distribution; AI governance: tiered regulation based on risk, mandatory audits, human-in-the-loop for high-stakes decisions; open-source AI for public-interest applications; international AI governance agreements. | High: technological change rate may exceed governance adaptation rate | Yes: simulate AI deployment scenarios and labor-market disruption |
| RSK-011 | Information Ecosystem Collapse | Post-truth dynamics in multiple democracies, Rwanda radio genocide, Myanmar Facebook violence | Shared factual basis for discourse erodes under misinformation, disinformation, propaganda, algorithmic amplification of outrage, media fragmentation, declining trust in expertise. Democratic deliberation becomes impossible. | 7 | Media literacy from primary school; public-service media with independence guarantees; platform accountability with algorithmic transparency; adversarial-content flagging with appeal; support for investigative journalism; deliberative-democracy mechanisms that build shared information base; digital information authentication infrastructure. | Medium-High: information warfare is asymmetric (cheap to attack, expensive to defend) | Yes: simulate information-ecosystem degradation and recovery dynamics |
| RSK-012 | Demographic Collapse (Fertility Below Replacement) | Japan, South Korea, Italy, much of Eastern Europe | Fertility falls far below replacement; aging population with shrinking workforce; intergenerational fiscal crisis; care infrastructure overwhelmed; eventual population decline. | 7 | Universal childcare, parental leave, and family support; housing affordability for young families; immigration where culturally sustainable; care-work recognition and compensation; social-design for aging society (housing, community, purpose); avoid coercion in family policy -- this is a design constraint, not a failure signal if freely chosen. | Medium: some decline is by choice, not failure; but extreme decline creates cascading effects | Yes: simulate demographic trends and institutional response adaptability |
| RSK-013 | Internal Fragmentation / Secession or Civil War | Yugoslavia, Sudan, U.S. Civil War, Spanish Civil War, Syrian Civil War | Regional, ethnic, religious, or ideological factions lose commitment to shared institutions; conflict escalates from political to violent; external actors fuel division. | 9 | Federal/confederal structure that grants genuine autonomy; constitutional secession procedures with high thresholds (e.g., supermajority + cooling-off period); economic integration that makes separation costly; shared civic identity independent of ethnic/religious identity; international guarantees for minority rights; inter-community exchange and rotation programs. | Medium: identity-based conflict is deeply rooted | Yes: simulate factional divergence under varying federal/confederal arrangements |
| RSK-014 | Emergency Powers Creep / Permanent Crisis Governance | Patriot Act provisions still in force >20 years; various states of emergency extended by decades; COVID-era powers persisting past health justification | Temporary crisis powers become permanent as successive emergencies (real or manufactured) justify extension; civil liberties erode incrementally without any single "dictatorship moment." | 8 | Constitutional hard limits: emergency duration capped and non-renewable without supermajority; sunset clauses with automatic expiration; independent review body that must reauthorize; banned powers list (e.g., suspension of habeas corpus, censorship of political speech, postponement of elections); post-emergency mandatory review and remedy. | Medium-High: crisis psychology makes limits politically costly to enforce | Yes: simulate emergency scenarios and power reversion after crisis |
| RSK-015 | Care Crisis / Social Reproduction Collapse | Correlates: declining birthrates, elder-abuse scandals, caregiver burnout epidemic, loneliness crisis | The work of caring for children, elderly, disabled, and sick is economically invisible and socially undervalued; as family structures change and women enter paid workforce, unpaid care deficit grows; market-based care creates inequality. | 7 | Universal care infrastructure: childcare, eldercare, disability support, family leave; care-work professionalization with fair compensation; community-based care networks; social-design to reduce care burden (walkable neighborhoods, intergenerational housing, preventative health); care-economy accounting in national metrics. | Medium: cultural shift around care is slow | Yes: simulate care-provision adequacy under demographic stress scenarios |
| RSK-016 | Cyber Collapse / Digital Infrastructure Failure | NotPetya (2017), Colonial Pipeline (2021), Estonia (2007), various unreported critical-infrastructure intrusions | Cyberattack on critical infrastructure (grid, water, finance, communications, healthcare) cascades across systems; recovery time measured in weeks-to-months rather than days. | 8 | Air-gapped critical infrastructure where feasible; mandatory cybersecurity standards for essential services; redundancy and manual fallback for all critical digital systems; cyber-defense force with proactive threat-hunting; international cybersecurity cooperation; cyber-insurance market with security-baseline requirements; supply-chain cybersecurity requirements. | Medium-High: offense-dominance in cyber domain | Yes: simulate cascading cyber-failure scenarios |
| RSK-017 | Competence Collapse / Anti-Expertise Culture | Various populist movements, Lysenkoism in USSR, climate-science denial campaigns | Expertise delegitimized through political attacks, misinformation, or institutional capture; policy disconnected from evidence; preventable crises multiply because expert warnings ignored. | 7 | Science-guiding institutions with independence guarantees (like central-bank independence); adversarial scientific review as institutionalized norm; public-facing evidence synthesis (transparent uncertainty); science communication integrated with civic education; competence-gating for technical governance roles without creating technocratic oligarchy. | Medium: anti-expertise is politically useful to authoritarians | Simulate indirectly: coupled with RSK-011 (information ecosystem) |
| RSK-018 | Nuclear / Existential Catastrophe | Hiroshima/Nagasaki, Cuban Missile Crisis, near-misses (Stanislav Petrov, Able Archer 83), potential AI-driven escalation | Nuclear war, engineered pandemic, uncontrolled AI, or other existential-risk scenario. Civilization-ending or near-ending. | 10 | Nuclear non-proliferation and arms-control treaties; de-alerting and no-first-use policies; AI in nuclear command-and-control with human-in-the-loop; biosecurity governance and verification; existential-risk research and horizon-scanning; international crisis-communication hotlines. | High: low-probability but civilization-ending risk requires civilization-level response | Simulate only to degree ethically and technically feasible; primary mitigation is diplomatic/political, not simulation-based |
| RSK-019 | Observability Failure / Opacity Cascade | Soviet economic-data falsification, Enron accounting fraud, various authoritarian states | When institutions that measure and report on societal health are captured or corrupted, feedback loops break. Decision-makers operate on false data. Problems compound unseen until catastrophic failure. | 8 | Independent statistical agencies with publication autonomy; protected-access audit logs for government decisions; whistleblower protections with safe disclosure channels; decentralized observability (citizen-science, community-level data collection, media); automated anomaly detection on public-institution data streams. | Medium: requires cultural and institutional commitment to transparency | Yes: simulate opacity propagation through institutional networks |
| RSK-020 | Boundary Failure / Leaky External Interfaces | Colonial-era disease transmission, Stuxnet crossing air gap, financial contagion 2008, refugee crises overwhelming border infrastructure | External boundaries fail in one or more domains: physical (military incursion, uncontrolled migration), biological (pathogen entry), economic (financial contagion, supply disruption), informational (data exfiltration, propaganda penetration), ecological (pollution import, invasive species). | 8 | Explicit boundary design per domain; layered defense (not single wall); monitoring and early-warning at each boundary layer; managed permeability for beneficial flows (trade, migration, information) with safeguards for harmful flows; boundary-integrity metrics and regular audit; international cooperation on transboundary risks. | Medium-High: complete boundary control is impossible without totalitarian methods | Yes: simulate boundary-failure scenarios across domains |
