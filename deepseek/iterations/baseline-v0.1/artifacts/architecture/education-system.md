# ART-013: Education System Architecture

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`
Status: draft

## Purpose

Design an education system that produces resilient, capable, ethical, emotionally intelligent, practically skilled, and civically competent citizens across the entire lifespan. Education must develop people, not sort them; enable flourishing, not reproduce class hierarchy.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Education is a right, not a commodity**: Universal, free, high-quality education from early childhood through adult lifelong learning.
2. **Educate the whole person**: Cognitive, emotional, social, physical, practical, civic, and ethical development -- all are essential, none is optional.
3. **Intrinsic motivation over extrinsic sorting**: Education structured for autonomy, competence, and relatedness (BF-005), not credential competition.
4. **Multiple paths to capability**: Not everyone learns the same way or needs the same path. Multiple educational pathways are available without hierarchy of prestige.
5. **Equity through universal quality**: Every child receives education of sufficient quality that family wealth does not determine educational outcomes. This is achieved through adequate, equitable public funding, not through private supplementation.
6. **Education is lifelong**: Learning does not end at 18 or 22. The system supports learning from birth to death.

## Learning Phases

### Phase 1: Early Childhood (0-5 years)

**Infrastructure**:
- **Universal home visiting**: All families with children 0-3 receive regular home visits from early-childhood educators and health professionals (linked to MOD-HMH community health networks).
- **Universal early childhood education**: Free, high-quality childcare and preschool from age 6 months (linked to MOD-HMH and MOD-ECO care infrastructure). Available full-time or part-time.
- **Play-based curriculum**: No formal academic instruction before age 6. Focus: language development, social-emotional learning, gross and fine motor skills, creativity, nature connection, and relationship-building.
- **Early intervention**: Developmental screening and intervention integrated (speech, occupational therapy, behavioral support) -- free and accessible.

**Staff**: Early childhood educators with specialized degree, competitive compensation parity with K-12 teachers. Low child-to-adult ratios (3:1 for infants, 5:1 for toddlers, 8:1 for preschoolers).

### Phase 2: Primary Education (6-11 years)

**Structure**: Community-based primary schools serving 2-5 community cells (200-400 students). Small schools for relationship depth.

**Curriculum Core**:
1. **Literacy and Language Arts**: Reading, writing, oral communication, multiple languages (including heritage languages where community-demanded).
2. **Mathematics**: Numeracy, mathematical reasoning, real-world application, financial literacy.
3. **Science**: Inquiry-based, emphasizing scientific method, observation, and wonder. Environmental science, biology, physics, chemistry foundations.
4. **Civic Education**: How our community and society work. Rights and responsibilities. Democratic participation. Conflict resolution. Historical literacy (age-appropriate, honest about both achievements and failures).
5. **Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)**: Emotional vocabulary, self-regulation, empathy, cooperation, conflict resolution, recognizing and reporting unsafe situations.
6. **Physical Education and Health**: Daily physical activity, nutrition, body literacy, basic health knowledge, outdoor education.
7. **Arts and Creativity**: Music, visual arts, drama, dance, creative writing. Not optional extras -- fundamental to human development.
8. **Practical Skills**: Basic cooking, gardening, tool use, repair, digital literacy, emergency preparedness -- age-appropriate.

**Pedagogy**:
- Mixed-age classrooms (2-3 year spans) enabling peer teaching and flexible progression
- Project-based and inquiry-based learning
- Significant outdoor learning time
- Minimal standardized testing (diagnostic only, not high-stakes)
- No grading before age 10 (narrative assessments instead)
- Individual learning plans reviewed quarterly with family

### Phase 3: Secondary Education (12-18 years)

**Structure**: Regional secondary schools serving groups of primary schools (500-1000 students). Larger scale justified by specialist teacher needs and peer diversity.

**Continuation Core (all students, all years)**:
1. Advanced literacy, rhetoric, and media literacy
2. Mathematics through statistics, probability, and applied math
3. Sciences: biology, chemistry, physics, earth/environmental science
4. Civic and political literacy: governance systems, law, economics, philosophy, ethics
5. History: multiple perspectives, critical historiography, honest reckoning with difficult histories
6. Health and relationships: comprehensive sex education, consent, mental health, substance-use education
7. Physical education and sports (competitive and non-competitive options)
8. Arts (choice of discipline, continued through graduation)

**Specialization Strands (choose by interest, not tracking)**:
1. **Academic/Research**: Advanced sciences, mathematics, humanities -- university preparation
2. **Technical/Vocational**: Engineering, computer science, biotechnology, skilled trades, agriculture -- certification-integrated
3. **Arts and Design**: Music, visual arts, design, performance, digital media -- portfolio development
4. **Public Service**: Governance, law, public health, education, social work -- internship-integrated
5. **Entrepreneurship and Enterprise**: Business, cooperative management, social enterprise, innovation -- project-based

**Key Design Features**:
- Strands are of equal prestige. No "academic is better than vocational" hierarchy. University-bound and trade-bound students share core curriculum and school community.
- Strand choice at 15, not 11 (delay tracking to avoid reproducing class background through early sorting).
- Strand switching permitted. No dead ends.
- All strands include internship, apprenticeship, or community-project component.
- Assessment: portfolio-based, competency demonstration, capstone projects. Standardized assessments used for system-quality monitoring, not individual gatekeeping.

### Phase 4: Tertiary and Adult Education (18+)

**Post-secondary options** (all publicly funded, tuition-free, with living stipend):

1. **Universities**: Research and liberal education. Admission based on demonstrated capability and motivation, not test scores alone. Holistic review. Quotas for first-generation and underrepresented students.
2. **Polytechnics and Technical Institutes**: Applied education in engineering, technology, healthcare, skilled trades. Industry and cooperative-integrated.
3. **Community Colleges**: Flexible, local, multiple pathways -- academic transfer, technical certification, adult basic education, continuing education.
4. **Apprenticeships**: Earn-while-you-learn model for skilled trades and technical professions. Cooperative sector and public-sector apprenticeship commitments.
5. **Cooperative Universities**: Worker-owned, student-governed higher education (Mondragon University model -- BCM-007).

**Lifelong Learning Entitlement**: Every adult entitled to 2 years of publicly funded education/training across lifetime (DEC-ECO-005). Usage tracked through individual learning accounts. Covers tuition + living stipend. Can be used in segments (not all at once).

## Civic Education: Deep Design

Civic education is not one subject but a thread through all phases:

**Age 6-11**: How does our classroom community make decisions? What do community council meetings look like? What rights do I have? How do I resolve conflicts fairly? Visit community institutions.

**Age 12-18**: Study of governance systems, constitutional values, history, economics, law, ethics. Student governance with real authority over school decisions. Participation in youth citizen assemblies. Mandatory community service (healthcare, environment, elder support, civic organizations). Internship in public service strand.

**Adult**: Civic literacy easily accessible. Deliberative democracy training for citizen assemblies. Governance literacy for sortition-selected citizens.

**Media and Information Literacy** (all levels): How to evaluate sources, recognize misinformation, understand algorithmic curation, identify propaganda, practice epistemic humility, and engage constructively with disagreement.

## Teacher Architecture

### Selection
- Competitive selection into teacher-education programs based on academic capability, emotional intelligence, communication skills, and demonstrated commitment to education. Not purely test-score-based.
- Diverse recruitment: proactive outreach to underrepresented communities. Teaching force should reflect student population.

### Education and Training
- 4-year university degree + 2-year teaching residency (paid, mentored classroom placement with gradual responsibility).
- Curriculum: child development, learning science, subject-matter expertise, pedagogy, classroom management, trauma-informed practice, special education, cultural competency, SEL facilitation.
- Continuing professional development: mandatory 10 days per year, self-directed within framework. Peer learning communities. Sabbatical option every 7 years.

### Compensation and Working Conditions
- Competitive compensation: at or above national professional median.
- Manageable workload: maximum 20 hours/week classroom contact time. Remaining time for preparation, collaboration, professional development, family communication.
- Small class sizes: maximum 20 students (primary), 25 (secondary).
- Classroom support: teaching assistants for large or high-need classes.
- Mental health support for teachers: teaching is emotionally demanding. Counseling, peer support, and manageable workload are structural, not optional.

### Evaluation
- Multi-source: peer observation, student feedback (age-appropriate), administrator observation, learning-outcome data (contextualized, not raw test scores).
- Support-oriented, not punitive. Professional growth plans before performance management.
- Removal process for incompetent or harmful teachers: fair, evidence-based, with appeal.

### Professional Autonomy
- Teachers have significant professional autonomy within national curriculum frameworks.
- Curriculum developed collaboratively by teachers, subject-matter experts, and community representatives -- not dictated by political leadership.
- National curriculum framework sets essential capabilities; pedagogical method, materials selection, and adaptation to local context are teacher decisions.

## Assessment Architecture

**Purposes**: Support learning (primary), certify capability (secondary), monitor system quality (tertiary). Never: rank children, sort into tracks, determine life opportunities, evaluate teachers punitively, create high-stakes anxiety.

**Methods**:
- **Portfolio assessment**: Collection of student work demonstrating growth and capability. Student-reflective component.
- **Competency demonstrations**: Students demonstrate skills and knowledge in authentic contexts (presentations, projects, performances, experiments, teaching others).
- **Narrative assessments**: Teacher-written descriptions of student development. Shared with families.
- **Capstone projects**: Major self-directed project demonstrating integration of learning (secondary graduation requirement).
- **Standardized assessments**: Sample-based (not universal), used for system monitoring. Not used for individual student decisions. Assess critical thinking, not rote memorization.
- **No high-stakes exit exams**: University and career access based on portfolio, interview, demonstrated capability, not single test score.

## Preventing Class Reproduction

The education system is designed to prevent becoming a class-reproduction engine (CLM-021):

1. **Fully public, equitably funded**: No private schools receiving public funds. Per-pupil funding equalized across communities with additional weighting for disadvantage (weighted student funding).
2. **No financial barriers**: All education free including materials, meals, transport, extracurriculars, field trips.
3. **School integration**: Schools serve heterogeneous communities. If residential segregation exists, school catchment areas drawn to maximize socioeconomic diversity.
4. **Late tracking**: No formal tracking before age 15. All students share core through secondary.
5. **Strand parity**: Equal resources, prestige, and pathways from all secondary strands.
6. **University access reform**: Holistic admission. Quotas for underrepresented backgrounds. Free tuition + living stipend removes financial barrier.
7. **Adult second chances**: Lifelong learning entitlement. Adult basic education. Career changers and late bloomers always have paths.

## Evidence and Decisions

| Decision ID | Scope | Decision | Rationale | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| DEC-EDU-001 | Early childhood model | Universal, play-based, birth-to-5, integrated health-education | Evidence consistently shows early childhood investment has highest social return. Play-based approach superior to early academic instruction for long-term outcomes. | SRC-019 (ACEs), BF-007 (trauma prevention), substantial early-childhood literature |
| DEC-EDU-002 | Assessment model | Portfolio and competency-based; no high-stakes standardized testing for individuals | High-stakes testing narrows curriculum, increases anxiety, and advantages test-prep-able students from wealthy backgrounds. Portfolio/competency assessment supports learning and reduces class-reproduction (CLM-021). | SRC-052 (TYRANNY OF MERIT CRITIQUE) |
| DEC-EDU-003 | Tracking timing | Age 15, with equal-prestige strands and switching | Late tracking reduces class reproduction compared to early tracking. Equal prestige across strands prevents "academic" becoming upper-class pathway. | PISA data on tracking systems; CLM-021 |
| DEC-EDU-004 | Private schools | No public funding for private schools; all education through public system | Private education is primary class-reproduction mechanism. Public funding of private options undermines universal-quality public system (Nordic experience -- BCM-005). Right to private education at own expense preserved (VAL-010 pluralism) but not state-funded. | BCM-005, SRC-052 |
