# ART-017: Defense and External Relations Architecture

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`
Status: draft

## Purpose

Define how the society survives external threats while preserving its values, legitimacy, and internal freedoms. Design deterrence, defense, diplomacy, trade, migration, and crisis governance that protects without becoming what it defends against.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Defense preserves what it protects**: A military that destroys the society's values to defend its territory has failed. Constitutional constraints apply to defense as to all governance.
2. **Multi-domain, multi-layered defense**: Threats span physical, cyber, economic, informational, and biological domains. Defense must span all domains at all scales.
3. **Resilience through depth, not walls**: A resilient society is harder to attack successfully than a walled one. Distributed systems, redundancy, civil-society integration, and international alliances provide deterrence.
4. **External engagement is strategic, not ideological**: Trade, diplomacy, and alliances are judged by their contribution to security, resilience, and values, not by ideological purity. Engagement with non-democracies is sometimes necessary; it must be eyes-open.
5. **Civilian control, democratic oversight, constitutional limits**: Military and security services are subordinate to civilian governance. Budgets, operations, and doctrine subject to legislative oversight. Intelligence services independently overseen.

## Defense Architecture

### Conventional Defense

**Doctrine**: Defensive defense with deterrent capability. Military structured to defend territory and population, not to project power abroad. No expeditionary offensive capability beyond alliance obligations.

**Force Structure**:
- **Professional core**: Small, highly trained professional military for high-tech domains (air defense, cyber, special operations, intelligence, logistics, command).
- **Citizen reserve**: Universal (opt-out for conscientious objection) service option: military reserve, civil defense corps, or civil service corps. 6-12 months initial training, followed by periodic refresher. Modeled on Swiss/Finnish systems (CLM-034 debated but defensively successful).
- **Territorial defense units**: Community-based defense units integrated with civil society. Local knowledge, rapid mobilization, local defense.
- **Total force**: Sufficient to make invasion costs prohibitive.

**Civil-Military Relations**:
- **Civilian Minister of Defense**: Civilian, not former military. Appointed by National Executive, confirmed by National Assembly.
- **Parliamentary Defense Committee**: Cross-party oversight of military budget, doctrine, procurement, operations. Access to classified information through cleared members.
- **Inspector General of Defense**: Independent oversight of military conduct, procurement integrity, and compliance with constitutional values. Reports to Parliament, not Ministry.
- **Military ombuds**: Independent body for service-member complaints, whistleblowing, and rights protection.
- **Regular defense reviews**: Public defense white paper every 4 years. Doctrine, threats, capabilities, and budget transparently assessed.

**Defense Ethics**:
- Military oath to constitution, not to individuals, party, or government.
- Training in laws of armed conflict, rules of engagement, and ethical decision-making.
- Explicit prohibition on: torture, extrajudicial killing, collective punishment, targeting civilians.
- Duty to disobey manifestly unlawful orders (Nuremberg principle). Protection for those who refuse unlawful orders.
- Post-conflict mental health support and moral-injury care.

### Nuclear Policy
- Policy dependent on geopolitical context. Baseline assumes membership in defensive alliance with nuclear umbrella OR multilateral nuclear-disarmament framework.
- No independent nuclear weapons development in baseline.
- Active support for nuclear non-proliferation, arms control, de-alerting, and eventual disarmament.
- Policy direction: nuclear weapons are an existential risk to civilization (RSK-018). The only long-term solution is elimination. The short-term strategy depends on the behavior of nuclear-armed states.

### Civil Defense
- **Civil defense corps**: Universal service option. Trained in disaster response, emergency medicine, infrastructure repair, community support, and emergency communications.
- **Community resilience plans**: Every community cell has documented civil-defense plan: shelter locations, emergency supplies, communications protocols, mutual-aid activation.
- **Public warning system**: Multi-channel emergency alert (mobile, broadcast, sirens, digital). Redundant. Tested regularly.
- **Strategic reserves**: Food, water, medicine, fuel, and essential materials for 12-month disruption (MOD-ECOINF).
- **Continuity of governance**: Designated succession, dispersed authority, secure communications, constitutional continuity.

### Cyber Defense
- **National Cyber Defense Agency**: Civilian agency responsible for defending government and critical infrastructure networks.
- **Active defense**: Hunt forward operations to identify threats before they reach critical systems.
- **Offensive cyber capability**: For deterrence and defense. Use governed by same constitutional and legal constraints as other military capabilities. Civilian control. Legislative oversight. Parliamentary committee must authorize offensive operations above threshold.
- **Cyber Civic Reserve**: Skilled civilian technologists who can be mobilized for national cyber defense during crisis.
- **Critical infrastructure cybersecurity**: Per MOD-TAI standards.

## Diplomacy and Foreign Policy

### Principles
1. **Principled pragmatism**: Advance values (human rights, democracy, ecological stewardship, peace) while recognizing geopolitical realities. Trade-offs are explicit, not hidden.
2. **Multilateralism**: Strengthen international institutions, law, and cooperation. Address global problems (climate, pandemics, AI governance, nuclear proliferation) through multilateral frameworks.
3. **Alliances for defense and values**: Membership in defensive alliance with fellow democracies. Mutual defense commitments credible and resourced.
4. **Diplomatic engagement with all**: Maintain diplomatic relations even with non-democracies. Dialogue, negotiation, and engagement where possible. No diplomatic recognition of territorial annexation by force.
5. **Development and humanitarian assistance**: Contribute 0.7%+ of national income to international development and humanitarian assistance. Not tied to commercial or geopolitical interests.
6. **Climate diplomacy**: Active leadership in international climate cooperation. Support for climate-vulnerable nations. Climate-migration preparedness.

### Trade Policy
- **Fair trade, not just free trade**: Trade agreements must include labor standards, environmental protections, and human-rights provisions.
- **Strategic autonomy for essentials**: Trade liberalization for non-essential goods and services. Strategic autonomy for essentials (food, energy, medicine, critical technology, defense).
- **Supply-chain diversification**: Reduce dependency on any single country for critical imports. Herfindahl-index monitoring (VAL-004).
- **Trade enforcement**: Effective mechanisms for enforcing trade-agreement provisions, including labor and environmental standards.
- **Technology transfer**: Support technology transfer to developing countries for climate adaptation, public health, and sustainable development.

### Sanctions Policy
- Sanctions as targeted tool, not broad punishment (CLM-038 on sanctions ineffectiveness).
- Targeted sanctions on individuals (asset freezes, travel bans) over broad sectoral sanctions that harm civilian populations.
- Humanitarian exemptions mandatory.
- Multilateral sanctions preferred over unilateral. Unilateral sanctions only when multilateral not achievable and national interest or values require.
- Regular review: all sanctions programs reviewed annually for effectiveness, humanitarian impact, and continued justification.

## Migration and Asylum Architecture

### Principles
1. **Right to seek asylum**: Refugee Convention obligations. Non-refoulement (no return to persecution).
2. **Managed migration for mutual benefit**: Immigration policy balances demographic, economic, humanitarian, and social-cohesion considerations.
3. **Integration commitment**: Migration without integration policy fails both migrants and host society.
4. **Border governance that is secure, humane, and lawful**: Borders are governed, not militarized. Migration is managed, not criminalized.

### Asylum System
- **Right to apply**: Any person arriving may apply for asylum. Application assessed individually, fairly, with legal representation and appeal.
- **Processing**: Fair and efficient. Detention only as last resort for identified flight risk or security concern, with judicial review.
- **Integration from day one**: Asylum seekers may work, access healthcare and education, and participate in community while application is processed. Support, not exclusion, is the default.
- **Community sponsorship**: Communities may sponsor refugee families (successful Canadian model). Community provides housing, integration support, and welcome.
- **Resettlement**: Annual refugee-resettlement quota based on capacity. Prioritized for most vulnerable (UNHCR referrals).
- **Safe and legal routes**: Expand humanitarian visas, family reunification, and labor mobility to reduce dangerous irregular migration.

### Regular Migration
- **Points-based system**: Skills, family connections, humanitarian need, and diversity.
- **Community nomination**: Communities may nominate migrants for residence based on local needs and integration capacity.
- **Temporary labor migration**: Carefully regulated to prevent exploitation. Equal labor rights for migrant workers. Path to permanent residence.
- **Integration support**: Language training, civic orientation, employment support, community connection. Funded adequately.
- **Citizenship path**: Clear, predictable path to citizenship. Residency requirement, language, civic knowledge, good character. No arbitrary barriers. Dual citizenship permitted.

### Border Governance
- **Secure but humane**: Borders are enforcement points, not militarized zones. Technology-assisted screening (privacy-protective per CLM-032). Professional border force, not military.
- **Search and rescue**: Obligation to rescue persons in distress at sea or in transit. No criminalization of humanitarian assistance to migrants.
- **International cooperation**: Cooperation with origin and transit countries on migration management, development, and safe-return agreements (for those not qualifying for protection).
- **External boundaries**: Explicitly designed per VAL-019. Physical, data-exchange, trade, migration, biosecurity, and sensitive-knowledge boundaries defined and audited.

## Emergency and Crisis Powers (Defense-Specific)

Per MOD-GOV emergency-powers architecture (DEC-GOV-004). Defense-specific additions:

### Crisis Triggers
- Armed attack or imminent threat of armed attack
- Declaration of war by National Assembly (3/4 vote)
- National emergency declaration (DEC-GOV-004 process)

### Crisis Powers
- Military mobilization of reserves
- Requisition of private property for defense (with mandatory compensation at fair market value + 25% premium)
- Temporary restrictions on movement in combat zones
- Censorship of military-operational information only (not political speech)
- Prioritization of defense production
- Cyber counter-measures

### Crisis Constraints
- All crisis powers subject to MOD-GOV emergency-powers framework (30-day renewable, independent review, automatic expiration).
- Declaration of war requires 3/4 National Assembly. No executive war-making authority.
- Military conscription for combat roles requires separate legislative authorization (2/3 vote) and conscientious-objection accommodation.
- No suspension of elections during crisis.
- Independent war-crimes investigation capacity.
- Post-conflict mandatory review and accountability.

## Information Warfare Defense

### Resilience Architecture
- **Media literacy**: Universal competency from primary school through adult education. Inoculation against common disinformation techniques (pre-bunking). Critical thinking in digital contexts.
- **Public-service media**: Independent, well-funded public broadcasting with editorial independence guaranteed (similar to BBC/CBC/ABC model, but with strengthened independence protections). Not state media, not commercial-dependent.
- **Platform accountability**: Content-moderation transparency. Algorithmic-amplification disclosure. Authenticated-identity options (not mandatory, but available). Bots and coordinated-inauthentic-behavior detection and removal.
- **Civil-society monitoring**: Support for independent fact-checking organizations, investigative journalism, and disinformation research.
- **Social cohesion**: Strong communities, trusted institutions, and shared civic identity are the best defense against information warfare. Division is the vulnerability.
- **Strategic communications**: Government communicates transparently and competently, building trust. Counters foreign information operations not through censorship but through transparency, fact-based rebuttal, and exposure of techniques.

### What NOT To Do
- Ministry of Truth / government truth-determination body
- Censorship of domestic political speech as "disinformation"
- Criminalization of false speech (with narrow exceptions: defamation, fraud, incitement to violence -- already covered by existing law)
- Mandatory government narrative
- Surveillance of citizens' information consumption

The society must be resilient to lies, not attempt to eliminate them through state power. A government that decides what is true is a greater threat than false information.
