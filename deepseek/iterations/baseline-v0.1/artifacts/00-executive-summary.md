# ART-001: Executive Summary

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`
Status: draft

## Purpose

This iteration establishes the complete baseline framework for a resilient, evidence-grounded civilization architecture. It is the first run of the full research program defined in `prd.md`.

## What Changed in This Iteration

This is the initial baseline. All 32 artifacts are new. The framework now has:

- **5 research artifacts**: 100-source register, 50-claim evidence matrix, 14-benchmark catalog, 20-entry failure mode register, 13-point behavioral foundations document.
- **11 architecture artifacts**: value constitution (19 values formalized), governance system (3-tier federated hybrid), economic system (commons + cooperative + entrepreneurial + care layers), law and justice system (4-pathway), health and mental health system (community-embedded universal), education system (birth-to-lifelong), community and culture (Dunbar-scale cells), ecology and infrastructure (planetary boundaries framework), technology and AI governance (risk-tiered), defense and external relations (defensive deterrence), and cross-module technical integration architecture.
- **5 simulation artifacts**: ABM blueprint, model parameters in YAML, 10 stress-test scenarios, validation criteria with thresholds, and initial validation report (simulation not yet executed).
- **3 implementation artifacts**: phased roadmap, adoption playbook, governance migration plan.
- **6 review artifacts**: decision log (40+ decisions), assumption register (30+ assumptions), contradiction register (7 value + 8 operational), risk register (20 risks assessed), adversarial review (6 perspectives, 30 findings), and change log.
- **2 summary artifacts**: this executive summary and the framework manifesto.

## Highest-Confidence Findings

1. **Inclusive institutions work** (CLM-001, High): The evidence that distributed political power, rule of law, and restrained elite extraction produce more durable and prosperous societies is among the most robust findings in political economy.

2. **Planetary boundaries are hard constraints** (CLM-005, High): Operating within ecological limits is not optional for long-term civilizational viability. The science is clear and the framework treats this accordingly.

3. **Structural anti-corruption beats awareness campaigns** (CLM-043, High): Independent prosecution, public procurement transparency, asset declarations, and enforcement independence are the mechanisms that actually work against corruption.

4. **Community-embedded governance scales better than centralized** (Ostrom design principles, CLM-002, High): Self-governing communities with clear boundaries, participatory decision-making, monitoring, graduated sanctions, and conflict-resolution mechanisms are empirically the most effective model for managing shared resources and community life.

5. **Universal healthcare produces better outcomes at lower cost** (CLM-022, High): The evidence strongly favors single-payer universal systems over market-based alternatives.

## Most Important Unresolved Questions

1. **Simulation not executed**: The entire simulation layer (5 artifacts) is specification, not results. The framework has not been computationally validated. This is the single most important next step.

2. **Implementation feasibility**: The transition from current governance to framework governance is acknowledged as the hardest problem. The implementation roadmap is detailed but speculative. Political feasibility, transition economics, and opposition dynamics are critical unknowns.

3. **Behavioral transition**: The framework assumes behavioral conditions (high trust, cooperation, civic engagement) that are not universal. How to transition from low-trust, low-cooperation baselines to framework-compatible baselines is unsolved.

4. **Cross-cultural applicability**: Behavioral assumptions draw primarily from WEIRD populations. Cross-cultural validity is uncertain.

5. **Ecological external dependency**: The framework can control its internal ecological footprint but cannot control global ecological conditions. External ecological collapse would stress the framework severely.

## Major Risks and Contradictions

**Highest residual risk**: Ecological overshoot (RSK-003, Overall: High). Global planetary boundaries are already transgressed. The framework can manage internal consumption but cannot prevent external ecological collapse from affecting the society.

**Irreducible tensions**: Seven value-level contradictions are documented (CON-VAL-001 through CON-VAL-007). These are not bugs but genuine tensions that must be managed, not resolved. The most significant are rights vs. resilience during crisis, and observability vs. privacy.

**Existential risk**: Nuclear/existential catastrophe (RSK-018) has low annual probability but civilization-ending consequence. The framework can reduce risk through diplomacy and arms control but cannot eliminate it.

**Implementation gap**: The distance between architectural design and operational society. The framework provides a detailed design but the path from here to there is the least specified and most uncertain element.
