# Governance System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-GOV`

## Design Thesis

The baseline governance model is a constitutional federation with local autonomy, regional coordination, central rights protection, deliberative citizen bodies, expert review, and adversarial audit. It rejects single-leader reliance and treats power concentration as a predictable failure mode rather than an ethical accident.

Evidence links: CLM-002, CLM-003, CLM-004, CLM-011, CLM-034.

## Institutional Layers

| Layer ID | Layer | Primary Powers | Constraints |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| GOV-L1 | Community cell | Local services, care coordination, dispute triage, preparedness, public deliberation, local budget proposals. | Must obey rights floor, anti-discrimination law, appeal rights, audit logs, exit rights. |
| GOV-L2 | Regional federation | Infrastructure, hospitals, watershed systems, courts of first appeal, regional economic planning, mutual aid reserves. | Regional decisions require public rationale and conflict review. |
| GOV-L3 | Constitutional tier | Rights enforcement, macroeconomic rules, external relations, ecological caps, national standards, anti-capture enforcement. | Enumerated powers, judicial review, transparent emergency limits. |
| GOV-L4 | Independent review layer | Audit, ombuds, inspector-general, electoral integrity, ethics enforcement, model review. | Multi-source appointment, term limits, budget independence, public reports. |

## Bodies

| Body ID | Body | Composition | Authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| GOV-B001 | Elected Assembly | Proportional representatives with term limits. | Budget, lawmaking, executive oversight, treaty approval. |
| GOV-B002 | Citizens' Deliberative Chamber | Stratified sortition, rotating terms, expert access, public facilitation. | Review legislation, trigger delay-and-reconsider votes, initiate public inquiries. |
| GOV-B003 | Technical Review Colleges | Licensed or validated experts plus public-interest members. | Publish impact assessments and uncertainty reports; no final authority over value tradeoffs. |
| GOV-B004 | Executive Service | Professional civil service and elected executive council. | Implements law under audit and removal procedures. |
| GOV-B005 | Adversarial Review Office | Independent investigators, auditors, security-cleared civil-liberties counsel. | Inspect institutions, publish findings, litigate against government, trigger emergency review. |
| GOV-B006 | Constitutional Court Network | Independent courts with transparent appointment and recusal rules. | Rights review, intergovernmental disputes, emergency power review. |

## Legitimacy and Competence

- Elected bodies decide value tradeoffs and priorities.
- Sortition bodies test public reason, identify blind spots, and slow capture-prone legislation.
- Expert colleges assess factual claims, implementation risks, and model limits.
- No expert body may permanently override constitutional rights or public accountability.
- Competence gates must be audited for predictive validity and adverse impact.

## Observability

Members can inspect:

- Budgets, procurement, voting records, conflicts of interest, audit trails, enforcement statistics, appeal outcomes, public-service performance, ecological accounts, emergency declarations, and unresolved risk registers.

Restricted data requires:

- Legal basis, data minimization, access log, independent audit, expiration, and appeal where disclosure is denied.
