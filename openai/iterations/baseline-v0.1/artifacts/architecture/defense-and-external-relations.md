# Defense and External Relations

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-DEF`

## Design Thesis

The society needs secure external boundaries without becoming isolationist or internally militarized. Defense, diplomacy, migration, trade, cyber resilience, and emergency preparedness must remain subordinate to rights and civilian accountability.

Evidence links: CLM-009, CLM-024, CLM-026, CLM-027, CLM-032, CLM-034.

## Threat Model

| Threat ID | Threat | Baseline Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| DEF-T001 | Natural disaster and climate shock | Regional mutual aid, reserve systems, resilient infrastructure, Sendai-style risk governance. |
| DEF-T002 | Pandemic or biosecurity event | Public health surge, lawful isolation powers, transparent evidence review, supply reserves. |
| DEF-T003 | Cyberattack | Critical-infrastructure segmentation, backups, incident response, public recovery metrics. |
| DEF-T004 | Disinformation and institutional sabotage | Media literacy, public records, independent audit, platform transparency, narrow security investigations. |
| DEF-T005 | Military coercion | Defensive doctrine, alliances, civil defense, protected infrastructure, civilian control. |
| DEF-T006 | Supply-chain cutoff | Strategic reserves, local production floors, diversified trade. |
| DEF-T007 | Migration shock | Rights-based processing, regional burden sharing, integration capacity, anti-exploitation safeguards. |
| DEF-T008 | Coup or elite capture | Distributed command, legality requirements, whistleblower channels, independent courts, emergency review. |

## External Boundary Design

| Boundary | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Physical access | Transparent migration categories, asylum protections, health/security screening with appeal. |
| Trade | Diversify critical imports, ban forced-labor supply chains, preserve strategic reserves. |
| Data exchange | Cross-border data transfers require rights equivalence or binding safeguards. |
| Cyber | Zero-trust critical systems, incident disclosure, and international cyber norms. |
| Biosecurity | Tiered access to dangerous capabilities, audit, and response protocols. |
| Sensitive knowledge | Narrow controls with review to prevent overbroad censorship. |

## Emergency Doctrine

- Civilian authority controls defense institutions.
- Secrecy is narrow, logged, and reviewable.
- Emergency powers expire unless renewed under heightened thresholds.
- Public after-action reports are required, with classified appendices reviewed independently.
