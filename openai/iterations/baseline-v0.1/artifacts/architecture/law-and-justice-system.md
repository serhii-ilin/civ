# Law and Justice System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-LAW`

## Design Thesis

The legal order makes rights operational through public law, accessible remedies, independent courts, constrained enforcement, restorative options, protective separation when necessary, and strong limits on emergency powers.

Evidence links: CLM-005, CLM-006, CLM-014, CLM-015, CLM-033.

## Rights Floor

Non-derogable baseline rights:

- Bodily integrity and freedom from torture, slavery, and arbitrary detention.
- Due process, counsel, appeal, and public legal standards.
- Freedom of conscience, expression, association, religion, nonreligion, and dissent.
- Equal protection and non-discrimination.
- Privacy, data rights, and protection from generalized surveillance.
- Whistleblower, journalism, and public-interest disclosure protections.
- Exit rights from local communities and associations.

## Justice Pathways

| Pathway ID | Pathway | Use Case | Safeguards |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LAW-P001 | Local mediation | Low-risk interpersonal disputes. | Voluntary participation, trained mediators, appeal to formal process. |
| LAW-P002 | Restorative process | Harm with accountable participant and consenting harmed party. | No coercion, trauma-informed facilitation, exclusion for intimidation or severe danger. |
| LAW-P003 | Civil adjudication | Contract, property, family, administrative, rights claims. | Legal aid, public rules, appeal, accessible formats. |
| LAW-P004 | Criminal adjudication | Serious harm, coercion, violence, corruption, rights violation. | Presumption of innocence, counsel, evidence rules, independent judge/jury structure. |
| LAW-P005 | Protective separation | Ongoing danger that cannot be managed by lesser restrictions. | Periodic review, rehabilitation plan, humane conditions, appeal. |
| LAW-P006 | Institutional accountability | Abuse by public or essential-service institutions. | Independent investigation, public findings, remedies, personal liability where appropriate. |

## Public Safety Model

- Separate emergency response, investigation, adjudication, detention, and care.
- Use force only under necessity, proportionality, reporting, and independent review.
- All enforcement actions generate auditable records, with privacy controls for victims and minors.
- Public safety metrics include harm reduction, rights complaints, response time, clearance quality, use-of-force rate, appeal outcomes, and community trust.

## Emergency Powers

Emergency powers require:

- Specific declared threat.
- Legal basis and public rationale.
- Time limit.
- Least-restrictive means.
- Legislative renewal threshold.
- Judicial review.
- Compensation and remedy for abuse.
- Post-crisis audit.
