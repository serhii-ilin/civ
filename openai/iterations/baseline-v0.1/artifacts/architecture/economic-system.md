# Economic System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

## Design Thesis

The baseline economy is a mixed constitutional economy: markets for plural choice and innovation, commons and public provisioning for essentials, cooperative ownership where participation and local accountability matter, and binding ecological accounts for material throughput.

Evidence links: CLM-001, CLM-008, CLM-010, CLM-019, CLM-036.

## Economic Layers

| Layer ID | Layer | Included Goods and Functions | Governance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ECO-L1 | Survival floor | Basic nutrition, emergency shelter, primary care, emergency mental health, basic legal aid, water, sanitation, minimum energy, core connectivity. | Rights-backed universal access with service-level guarantees. |
| ECO-L2 | Commons infrastructure | Water, grid backbone, public data infrastructure, education, public health, core mobility, watershed protection. | Public, cooperative, or trust governance with user representation and audit. |
| ECO-L3 | Regulated markets | Consumer goods, non-essential services, housing construction, innovation markets, cultural production. | Competition law, labor standards, ecological pricing, consumer protection. |
| ECO-L4 | Contribution and ownership layer | Worker cooperatives, public-interest corporations, social enterprises, private firms, research ventures. | Plural ownership, anti-monopoly rules, transparency for concentrated power. |
| ECO-L5 | Strategic reserves | Food, energy, medicine, critical materials, disaster equipment, cyber recovery capacity. | Regional and central reserve mandates tied to risk models. |

## Tax and Rent Philosophy

| Revenue ID | Instrument | Rationale | Risks |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ECO-R001 | Land value and resource rent capture | Recovers unearned location and natural-resource gains for public use. | Assessment capture, local resistance. |
| ECO-R002 | Progressive income and wealth taxation | Limits extreme concentration and funds common goods. | Capital flight, avoidance, political backlash. |
| ECO-R003 | Pollution and material-throughput charges | Makes ecological cost visible and binding. | Regressive impact unless rebated. |
| ECO-R004 | Monopoly and automation rent taxation | Shares gains from network effects, intellectual monopolies, and automation. | Innovation disincentive if poorly designed. |
| ECO-R005 | Maintenance-dedicated infrastructure funds | Prevents visible expansion at the expense of hidden decay. | Budget rigidity. |

## Labor and Contribution

- Survival security is decoupled from labor coercion.
- Contribution is encouraged through wages, cooperative dividends, public-service pathways, care credits, skill grants, and recognition.
- Essential labor markets use staffing reserves, training pipelines, wage floors, and noncoercive civic-service options.
- Care work is counted in public accounts and eligible for support, respite, and pension credit.

## Anti-Concentration Rules

- No essential infrastructure monopoly may own its regulator, data layer, or dispute process.
- Market concentration triggers automatic review.
- Public procurement favors open standards and exit rights.
- Inheritance taxation and trust rules prevent durable oligarchy.
