# Ecology and Infrastructure System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-ECOINF`

## Design Thesis

Ecology and infrastructure form the hard constraint layer. The society must set ecological budgets, maintain critical redundancy, localize essential production where prudent, and design settlements for health, resilience, and low material waste.

Evidence links: CLM-009, CLM-022, CLM-023, CLM-024, CLM-025, CLM-026, CLM-027, CLM-028.

| Account ID | Account | Binding Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ECOINF-A001 | Carbon and greenhouse gases | Declining budget aligned with climate targets and adaptation planning. |
| ECOINF-A002 | Water | Watershed-level withdrawals must remain within replenishment and ecological-flow rules. |

| ECOINF-A005 | Food system | Food resilience metrics include local production floor, soil health, storage, nutrition, and affordability. |

## Infrastructure Standards

| System | Baseline Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Water | Multiple sources where feasible, watershed governance, leak control, sanitation redundancy, drought and flood plans. |
| Energy | Distributed generation, storage, demand response, critical-load islanding, grid cybersecurity, affordability protections. |
| Food | Regional production floors, storage reserves, regenerative soil standards, food access guarantees. |
| Housing | Safe density, mixed-income access, climate resilience, lifecycle maintenance, proximity to services. |
| Mobility | Walkable local access, public transit, emergency logistics, low-emission freight planning. |
| Communications | Resilient connectivity, public-interest digital infrastructure, offline fallback procedures. |
| Health infrastructure | Clinics, labs, cold chain, emergency shelters, clean air and heat-risk plans. |

## Resilience Targets

- Household readiness: 14 days for basic disruption where feasible.
- Community readiness: 30 to 90 days for food, water contingency, emergency power, and care coordination.
- Regional readiness: 6 to 24 months for critical reserves, repair capacity, health surge, and supply-chain substitution.

These targets are initial assumptions, not validated thresholds.
