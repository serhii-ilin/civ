# Education System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-EDU`

## Design Thesis

Education develops capability, judgment, emotional health, practical competence, civic agency, and lifelong adaptability. It is not only a credential pipeline.

Evidence links: CLM-019, CLM-020, CLM-021, CLM-035.

## Capability Model

| EDU-C001 | Literacy and numeracy | Adults can understand contracts, health instructions, public budgets, risk charts, and basic statistics. |

| EDU-C003 | Civic competence | Adults understand rights, local governance, public finance, deliberation, and appeal pathways. |

| EDU-C006 | Technical adaptability | Adults can reskill as technology and economic needs change. |
| EDU-C007 | Ethical agency | Learners can reason about tradeoffs, pluralism, power, and future generations. |

## System Design

- Early childhood support is universal and quality-audited.
- Core learning goals are constitutional-tier standards; pedagogy and cultural content are locally adaptable within rights limits.
- Assessment combines portfolios, performance tasks, exams where valid, and civic/practical demonstrations.
- Teachers are selected for domain competence, pedagogical skill, emotional maturity, and ethical fitness.
- Adult reskilling is funded as public infrastructure, especially during automation and climate transitions.

## Anti-Credentialism Controls

- Public roles require validated competence rather than degree-only filters.
- Apprenticeship and demonstration pathways must exist for major occupations.
- Assessment validity and adverse impact are audited.
- Education funding formulas correct for disadvantage rather than rewarding inherited advantage.
