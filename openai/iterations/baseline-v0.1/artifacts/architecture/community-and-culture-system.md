# Community and Culture System

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Module: `MOD-CUL`

## Design Thesis

Communities are the human-scale layer for belonging, care, early conflict repair, local preparedness, civic practice, and cultural pluralism. They must not become coercive identity containers.

Evidence links: CLM-018, CLM-019, CLM-021, CLM-028.

## Community Cell

Baseline range: 500 to 5,000 residents for primary community governance, with smaller neighborhood circles for mutual aid and larger regional bodies for services requiring scale. This is an assumption pending simulation and pilot testing.

## Pluralism Rules

- Communities may have distinct customs, calendars, and voluntary practices.
- No community may override constitutional rights, restrict exit, impose religious or ideological tests for public services, or conceal abuse.
- Minority cultural infrastructure receives protection when compatible with rights.
- Public rituals must include opt-out pathways and nonreligious alternatives.

## Observability Without Shame

Observable local data:

- Budget, service levels, response times, aggregated wellbeing indicators, conflict-resolution counts, care capacity, preparedness status, unresolved risks.

Prohibited local data practices:

- Public ranking of private persons, coerced disclosure of belief or health status, generalized household monitoring, and social-credit scoring.
