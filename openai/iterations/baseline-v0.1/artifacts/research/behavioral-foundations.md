## Behavioral Premises

| Premise ID | Statement | Evidence | Design Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BEH-001 | People cooperate more reliably when rules are seen as legitimate, enforceable, and locally intelligible. | CLM-001, CLM-002, CLM-004 | Governance must combine participation, monitoring, and fair enforcement. |
| BEH-002 | People are neither purely selfish nor reliably altruistic; institutional incentives, norms, and monitoring shape behavior. | CLM-001, CLM-011 | Design must avoid both cynical coercion and naive voluntarism. |
| BEH-003 | Chronic stress, isolation, trauma, and childhood adversity impair health, trust, learning, and civic capacity. | CLM-007, CLM-008, CLM-018 | Health, housing, education, and community design are civic infrastructure. |
| BEH-004 | Autonomy, competence, and relatedness support durable motivation. | CLM-019 | Education and work systems should use contribution pathways rather than coercive precarity. |
| BEH-005 | Childhood investment has unusually high leverage for long-run capability. | CLM-020, CLM-021 | Early childhood support, family support, and safe neighborhood design are baseline systems. |
| BEH-006 | People respond to institutions through perceived fairness, not just material outcomes. | CLM-004, CLM-005 | Appeals, explanations, and procedural fairness must be observable. |
| BEH-007 | Power tends to preserve itself when monitoring and removal are weak. | CLM-002, CLM-034 | Authority-bearing roles require rotation, audit, appeal, and independent challenge. |

## Design Heuristics

- Prefer systems that make good behavior easier than bad behavior.
- Treat shame, fear, and coercive dependency as high-risk control mechanisms.
- Keep local belonging compatible with exit rights and pluralism.
- Separate care, investigation, adjudication, and punishment functions.
- Design for ordinary humans under stress, not idealized citizens.
