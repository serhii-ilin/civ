# Failure Mode Register

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

## Failure Modes

| Risk ID | Failure Mode | Historical Examples | Mechanism | Mitigations | Residual Risk | Test |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| RSK-001 | Elite capture | Roman Republic, regulatory capture in modern states | Wealth, information, and appointment power compound into durable control. | Asset transparency, anti-monopoly rules, rotation, independent audit, campaign finance limits, public-interest litigation. | High | Simulate wealth-power feedback and audit failure rates. |
| RSK-002 | Competence gate becomes class gate | Exam-based bureaucracies, credential inflation | Selection criteria overfit to privileged preparation rather than real capability. | Multiple pathways, apprenticeship, blind assessment, periodic validity audits, appeal rights. | Medium | Audit demographic skew and predictive validity. |
| RSK-003 | Transparency becomes surveillance | Authoritarian social-credit systems, workplace monitoring | Institutional observability tools are repurposed to track private life. | Data minimization, privacy courts, warrant rules, public logs for institutional actors only. | High | Red-team data linkage and abuse paths. |
| RSK-004 | Sortition becomes symbolic | Advisory-only citizen panels | Deliberative bodies lack agenda power or government response duties. | Statutory response requirements, budget for independent experts, publication of dissenting reports. | Medium | Track adoption rate of recommendations and response latency. |
| RSK-005 | Commons layer underfunding | Infrastructure decay, public housing backlogs | Universal services lose maintenance funding and quality legitimacy. | Dedicated maintenance funds, service-level guarantees, transparent backlog metrics. | High | Stress fiscal downturn and deferred maintenance. |
| RSK-006 | Ecological accounting becomes decorative | Weak carbon pricing regimes | Costs are measured but not binding on decisions. | Hard caps, ecological budget courts, automatic permit tightening. | High | Trigger tests for boundary violations. |
| RSK-007 | Mental health support becomes coercive | Involuntary institutional abuse | Crisis systems prioritize order over autonomy and consent. | Due process, least-restrictive care, independent patient advocates, sunset reviews. | Medium | Audit involuntary intervention rates and appeals. |
| RSK-008 | Emergency powers normalize | Permanent states of exception | Crisis authorities find incentives to preserve expanded discretion. | Automatic expiration, judicial review, legislative supermajorities, compensation for abuse. | High | Simulate repeated crises and expiration compliance. |
| RSK-009 | External threat militarizes civic life | Cold-war security states | Defense readiness expands secrecy, hierarchy, and suspicion. | Civilian supremacy, narrow secrecy, periodic declassification, rights floor. | Medium | Inspect classified-area growth and rights complaints. |
| RSK-010 | AI governance becomes vendor capture | Proprietary public-sector systems | Public decisions depend on opaque tools controlled by contractors. | Open standards, audit rights, model cards, procurement rules, human appeal. | High | Procurement red-team and algorithm appeal outcomes. |
| RSK-011 | Local autonomy protects local abuse | Segregationist or corrupt localities | Subsidiarity prevents higher-tier intervention against rights violations. | Non-derogable rights, federal review, ombuds office, mobility support. | High | Rights-violation escalation drills. |
| RSK-012 | Value overload causes paralysis | Overconstitutionalized systems | Too many veto points and metrics block timely action. | Triage rules, emergency but bounded authority, periodic simplification reviews. | Medium | Measure decision latency under stress scenarios. |
