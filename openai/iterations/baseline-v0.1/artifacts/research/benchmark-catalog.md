# Benchmark Catalog

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

## Use Policy

Benchmarks are analogies and partial evidence, not templates. Each comparison must state mechanism, boundary conditions, and what would falsify the analogy.

## Institutional Benchmarks

| Benchmark ID | System | Candidate Lessons | Boundary Conditions | Related Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BEN-001 | Iroquois Confederacy | Federal coordination can coexist with local autonomy and durable diplomatic procedures. | Historical records are partial; scale, technology, and external pressures differ sharply from modern states. | CLM-002, CLM-011 |
| BEN-002 | Swiss Confederation | Federalism, subsidiarity, direct-democratic mechanisms, and militia-style civic obligations can support legitimacy. | Wealth, geography, history, and social trust are not portable assumptions. | CLM-003, CLM-004 |
| BEN-003 | Nordic social democracies | High public capacity plus universal services can coexist with market economies and comparatively high trust. | Small-to-medium high-income states; results depend on tax compliance, labor institutions, and political culture. | CLM-004, CLM-008 |
| BEN-004 | Singapore | Competent state capacity and long-term planning can produce strong infrastructure and public services. | Political liberties, media freedom, and replicability are contested; not a rights-complete model. | CLM-002, CLM-014 |
| BEN-005 | Mondragon cooperative network | Worker ownership and federation structures can scale beyond small communes. | Operates inside a broader market and state framework; sector-specific performance varies. | CLM-001, CLM-010 |
| BEN-006 | Kibbutzim | Communal provisioning can support strong social cohesion under shared identity and external pressure. | Generational change, privacy, individual autonomy, and economic modernization created adaptation pressure. | CLM-010, CLM-019 |
| BEN-007 | Auroville | Intentional communities can test pluralistic norms and alternative governance. | Limited scale, dependence on host-state law, internal conflict, and economic dependency limit generalization. | CLM-003, CLM-010 |
| BEN-008 | Roman Republic | Mixed institutions can restrain power for a time but fail under inequality, military personalization, and elite competition. | Ancient context; useful mainly as failure-mode analogy. | CLM-002, CLM-034 |
| BEN-009 | Byzantine Empire | Administrative continuity, diplomacy, strategic depth, and institutional adaptation can extend resilience. | Imperial hierarchy and coercion conflict with the value constitution. | CLM-027 |
| BEN-010 | Modern disaster-risk systems | Preparedness, redundancy, and recovery planning reduce avoidable harm when institutional responsibility is clear. | Disaster type, wealth, geography, and public compliance vary. | CLM-027, CLM-024 |
