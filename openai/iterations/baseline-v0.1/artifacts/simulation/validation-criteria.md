# Validation Criteria

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

## Validation Standard

Simulation can support redesign decisions, but it cannot validate moral legitimacy. Results must be paired with evidence review, legal review, stakeholder review, and adversarial critique.

## Pass Conditions

| Criterion ID | Criterion | Initial Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- |
| VALD-001 | Survival-floor access remains above target during ordinary conditions. | 99 percent access. |
| VALD-002 | Emergency powers expire or receive lawful review. | 100 percent of declarations. |
| VALD-003 | Rights violations do not increase under stress without review and remedy. | No unreviewed systemic increase. |
| VALD-004 | Ecological accounts remain inside hard caps under baseline growth. | No cap breach. |
| VALD-005 | Critical services recover within scenario-specific targets. | Water/health/energy targets met. |
| VALD-006 | Audit bodies detect capture before irreversible concentration. | Detection before threshold breach in most runs. |
| VALD-007 | Mental-health and care systems avoid persistent backlog after shocks. | Return to target within defined recovery window. |
| VALD-008 | Public trust remains recoverable after institutional error. | Trust recovers after transparent remedy. |

## Redesign Triggers

- A rights failure is repeated across scenarios.
- A single institution becomes a hidden point of failure.
- Ecological caps are breached under plausible demand.
- Commons quality declines enough to undermine legitimacy.
- Capture occurs faster than audit systems detect it.
- A model outcome depends on heroic compliance assumptions.
