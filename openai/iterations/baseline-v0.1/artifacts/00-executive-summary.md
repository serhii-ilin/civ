# Executive Summary

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

## Purpose

This baseline creates a complete first-pass civilization architecture research artifact set. It translates the PRD, canon, and module prompts into a draft framework that can be reviewed, simulated, revised, and compared in later iterations.

## What Changed in This Iteration

- Created research artifacts: source register, evidence matrix, benchmark catalog, failure-mode register, and behavioral foundations.
- Created architecture artifacts for values, governance, economy, law, health, education, community, ecology, technology, defense, and cross-module technical architecture.
- Created simulation artifacts for model design, parameters, stress scenarios, validation criteria, and validation readiness.
- Created implementation artifacts for pilot roadmap, adoption playbook, and governance migration.
- Created review artifacts: decision log, assumption register, contradiction register, risk register, adversarial review, and change log.

## Highest-Confidence Findings

- Durable institutions require more than formal rules; power, implementation capacity, legitimacy, monitoring, and correction mechanisms matter. Evidence: CLM-001, CLM-002, CLM-011.
- Mental health, childhood conditions, social determinants, housing, education, and care systems are civic infrastructure, not downstream social services. Evidence: CLM-007, CLM-008, CLM-016, CLM-018, CLM-020.
- Ecological and infrastructure constraints must be binding because climate, food, water, energy, and disaster risks affect every other module. Evidence: CLM-009, CLM-022, CLM-024, CLM-026, CLM-027.
- AI, data, and cyber systems need rights-based governance, auditability, appeal, and lifecycle risk management. Evidence: CLM-029, CLM-030, CLM-032, CLM-033.

## Most Important Unresolved Questions

- Can institutional observability be strong enough to prevent capture without becoming surveillance? See CON-001.
- Can competence gates improve public authority without reproducing class hierarchy? See ASM-002 and RSK-002.
- What exact fiscal model funds universal services, reserves, and infrastructure maintenance without undermining innovation or legitimacy? See ASM-003, ASM-004, and RSK-005.
- How should ecological caps be calibrated and enforced across different regions? See ASM-011 and CON-002.
- Which emergency powers are genuinely necessary, and how can review bodies resist deference under fear? See CON-003 and RSK-008.

## Major Risks and Contradictions

Highest residual risks:

- RSK-001: Elite capture.
- RSK-003: Transparency becomes surveillance.
- RSK-005: Commons layer underfunding.
- RSK-006: Ecological accounting becomes decorative.
- RSK-008: Emergency powers normalize.
- RSK-010: AI/vendor capture.
- RSK-011: Local autonomy protects local abuse.

Core contradictions:

- CON-001: Observability vs privacy/security.
- CON-002: Ecological pricing vs affordability.
- CON-003: Public health speed vs civil liberties under uncertainty.
- CON-004: Shared education standards vs pluralism.
- CON-005: Land stewardship vs property autonomy and mobility.

## Recommended Next Iteration

Run a targeted `simulation_update` and `adversarial_review` focused on:

- SCN-003: Elite capture.
- SCN-004: Pandemic with uncertain early evidence.
- SCN-010: Fiscal downturn plus infrastructure decay.

The next iteration should convert the draft parameters into an executable minimal model and deepen the legal/privacy design for CON-001 before any real pilot.
