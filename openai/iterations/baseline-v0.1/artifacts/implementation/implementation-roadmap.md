# Implementation Roadmap

Iteration: `baseline-v0.1`

Status: `draft`

Artifact: `ART-024`

## Minimum Viable Pilot

A pilot should be a voluntary civic-infrastructure district or institutional consortium, not a sovereignty claim. It should test observability, care coordination, local resilience, participatory budgeting, service-level guarantees, and privacy-preserving public metrics.

Minimum pilot scope:

- 500 to 5,000 participants.
- Public dashboard for institutional metrics.
- Local mutual aid and emergency reserve plan.
- Participatory budget process with citizen deliberation.
- Legal aid and dispute-resolution pathway.
- Primary care and mental-health referral partnerships.
- Education/reskilling programs.
- Data-rights charter and audit.

## 1-Year Path

- Establish steering charter, rights floor, data policy, and conflict-of-interest rules.
- Select pilot community or institutional network.
- Build source register, public dashboard, decision log, and service inventory.
- Run tabletop stress scenarios.
- Create independent review panel.

## 5-Year Path

- Expand to regional federation of pilots.
- Add shared procurement, energy resilience, food resilience, care infrastructure, and education pathways.
- Validate contribution incentives and commons financing.
- Publish annual adversarial reviews.
- Begin simulation-informed policy revisions.

## 20-Year Path

- Mature into a constitutional network of communities, regions, institutions, and public-interest infrastructure.
- Harmonize with existing law through municipal, cooperative, nonprofit, public-benefit, and intergovernmental structures.
- Maintain amendment procedures, external audits, and exit rights.

## Legal Dependencies

- Entity formation for pilots, review bodies, fiscal accounts, and public-interest infrastructure.
- Data protection, privacy, civil-rights, procurement, labor, health, education, housing, and emergency-management compliance.
- Administrative appeal and judicial-review pathways for rights-affecting decisions.

## Economic Dependencies

- Sustainable operating funds for universal service pilots, maintenance, reserves, care infrastructure, and independent audit.
- Fiscal stress testing before expanding service guarantees.
- Procurement rules that avoid vendor lock-in and preserve exit options.

## Governance Dependencies

- A legitimate pilot charter, conflict-of-interest registry, decision log, risk register, and independent review panel.
- Clear authority boundaries between community, partner institutions, and existing public agencies.
- Escalation paths for rights complaints, data misuse, service failures, and local capture.

## Community Onboarding

- Plain-language charter review before enrollment.
- Rights, privacy, exit, grievance, and data-use orientation.
- Language access, disability access, and asynchronous participation options.
- Newcomer mentorship without ideological screening.
- Consent-based care network enrollment.

## Dependency Summary

| Dependency Type | Dependency |
| --- | --- |
| Legal | Entity formation, data protection compliance, procurement law, labor law, civil-rights compliance. |
| Economic | Sustainable financing, reserve funds, service contracts, maintenance funding. |
| Governance | Legitimate charter, audit body, dispute process, conflict-of-interest enforcement. |
| Community | Consent, onboarding, language access, care networks, safety protocols. |
| Technical | Secure data platform, public dashboard, identity/access controls, backup systems. |

## Exit and Rollback

- Participants retain ordinary legal rights and exit from pilot services.
- Essential services require continuity plans before governance changes.
- Failed experiments trigger public after-action review and sunset.
