# MOD-ECOINF: Ecology and Infrastructure

## Purpose

Design the ecological, spatial, and infrastructure foundations required for a durable society.

## Design Requirements

- Food sovereignty and regenerative agriculture.
- Water security, watershed governance, sanitation, and flood/drought resilience.
- Energy independence, storage, demand management, and grid resilience.
- Housing, land tenure, density, zoning, mobility, and public space.
- Local production floors for essential goods.
- Circular material flows and waste minimization.
- Climate adaptation and disaster readiness.
- Ecological accounting integrated into governance and economic decisions.

## Core Questions

- RQ-ECOINF-001: What are the ecological red lines for settlement, industry, and consumption?
- RQ-ECOINF-002: What must be locally producible at household, community, regional, and national scales?
- RQ-ECOINF-003: How should land ownership, land value, and land use be governed?
- RQ-ECOINF-004: What infrastructure redundancy is required for 6, 12, and 24 months of disruption?
- RQ-ECOINF-005: How are ecological costs priced, capped, or prohibited?
