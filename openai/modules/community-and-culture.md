# MOD-CUL: Community and Culture

## Purpose

Define social architecture, civic identity, cultural pluralism, belonging, rituals, family support, and community-scale function.

## Design Requirements

- Minimum viable community size and composition.
- Functional community roles without rigid hierarchy.
- Inter-community relationships and resource-sharing protocols.
- Isolation resilience for 6-24 months.
- Culture of dissent, repair, grief, celebration, and public service.
- Protection for pluralism, minority cultures, religious practice, nonreligious practice, and voluntary association.
- Exit rights and mechanisms for moving between communities.
- Care infrastructure for children, elders, disabled people, caregivers, and people in crisis.
- Community-level visibility into shared obligations, resource flows, conflict-resolution outcomes, wellbeing signals, and institutional response times without exposing private personal data.

## Core Questions

- RQ-CUL-001: What is the minimum viable community cell?
- RQ-CUL-002: Which roles must exist locally for resilience?
- RQ-CUL-003: How are conflicts resolved before they become legal crises?
- RQ-CUL-004: What shared rituals or practices build cohesion without coercive ideology?
- RQ-CUL-005: How are newcomers, dissenters, and minority groups integrated without forced assimilation?
- RQ-CUL-006: Which community processes should be observable by members, and how are shame, coercion, and surveillance avoided?
