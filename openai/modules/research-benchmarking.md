# MOD-RES: Research and Benchmarking

## Purpose

Build the empirical and historical base for the civilization framework.

## Research Scope

- Historical civilizations with notable resilience or longevity, including the Roman Republic, Byzantine Empire, Iroquois Confederacy, Swiss Confederation, Nordic social democracies, and Singapore.
- Failed states and collapsed civilizations, with emphasis on structural failure modes.
- Intentional communities and institutional experiments, including kibbutzim, Auroville, cooperative economies, and Mondragon Corporation.
- Political frameworks: direct democracy, liquid democracy, epistocracy, sortition, technocracy, federalism, subsidiarity, constitutionalism, and deliberative democracy.
- Economic models: circular economy, commons-based peer production, doughnut economics, post-scarcity theory, MMT, Georgism, progressive taxation, automation taxation, universal basic services, UBI, and market systems.
- Behavioral foundations: prosocial behavior, trust formation, Dunbar-scale limits, psychological safety, adverse childhood experiences, self-determination theory, trauma-informed design, and cooperation at scale.

## Core Questions

- RQ-RES-001: Which historical systems endured, and what mechanisms plausibly contributed to their endurance?
- RQ-RES-002: Which civilization failure modes recur across contexts?
- RQ-RES-003: Which countermeasures have empirical support rather than only theoretical appeal?
- RQ-RES-004: Which findings are robust, and which are context-specific?

## Required Outputs

- `research/source-register.md`
- `research/evidence-matrix.md`
- `research/benchmark-catalog.md`
- `research/failure-mode-register.md`
- `research/behavioral-foundations.md`

## Quality Gates

- Separate historical analogy from causal evidence.
- Do not generalize from one civilization or intentional community without boundary conditions.
- Record contested interpretations rather than presenting them as settled.
