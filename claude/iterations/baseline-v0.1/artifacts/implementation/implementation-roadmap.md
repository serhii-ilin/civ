# Implementation Roadmap

Artifact ID: ART-024
Iteration: baseline-v0.1
Status: draft
Change type: new
Owner module: all architecture modules
Depends on: ART-018

## Purpose

Translate the framework architecture (ART-008 through ART-018) into a phased adoption path. Specify a minimum viable pilot (MVP), 1-year, 5-year, and 20-year horizons, dependencies (legal, economic, governance), community-onboarding approach, and exit / rollback mechanisms.

This roadmap assumes the framework is being adopted in some real polity (a new community, a region, a state, or by reform of an existing nation-state). It does not assume any specific starting condition; it specifies what would change and in what order.

## Adoption Modes

The framework can be adopted under three distinct modes:

### Mode A: Greenfield Pilot

- A new community is founded or carved out under an enabling legal frame.
- The framework is implemented as the constitution of the new community.
- Smaller scale; selection bias of self-selected participants.

### Mode B: Reform-by-Layers

- An existing polity adopts the framework incrementally, module by module, through normal legislative and constitutional channels.
- High realism; slower; more conflict.

### Mode C: Post-Crisis Reset

- After a major shock (collapse of prior regime, occupation, severe institutional failure), the framework is offered as a coherent alternative during reconstruction.
- Higher legitimacy under specific conditions; risk of crisis-pressure compromises.

This roadmap is mode-agnostic where possible; mode-specific notes are flagged.

## Minimum Viable Pilot (ART-024-MVP)

The MVP demonstrates that the framework's core dynamics function at small scale and under realistic constraints. Scale: 5,000-15,000 members; duration: 5 years; mode: A (Greenfield Pilot) or pilot within a larger Mode B reform.

### MVP Required Elements

- Constitutional document derived from `value-constitution.md` (ART-008), adapted to local context.
- Local Council (GOV-T1) with mixed elected and sorted membership.
- Local Manager (qualified executive) with multi-body confirmation.
- Local Ombuds.
- Local Conflict Forum.
- Public decision log (TAI-O1 analogue).
- Member-observable dashboard for local indicators (TAI-O2 analogue).
- Universal access to: primary care, primary and secondary education, basic housing, basic energy, basic transit, mental-health support.
- Land-use plan with ecological caps locally enforced.
- Restorative-justice capacity (LAW-CJ3 analogue).
- Annual capability-and-civic assessment.

### MVP Excluded (Deferred to Scale-Up)

- Independent central bank.
- Full antitrust apparatus.
- Sovereign-wealth fund.
- National-scale defense and external relations.
- Foundation-model AI regulation (beyond local application).

### MVP Indicators

- Universal-services coverage.
- Local decision-log completeness.
- Trust-in-local-institutions.
- Conflict-resolution capacity.
- Reserve durations (food, energy, medicine).
- Sortition participation rates.
- Capability outcomes for residents.

### MVP Decision Gates

End of year 5: pass / iterate / abandon decision based on:

- Indicators in green or yellow.
- No red lines breached.
- Adversarial-review external evaluation.
- Member-deliberation outcome (sortition body confirms continuation).

## Year-One Horizon (ART-024-Y1)

If the MVP is the local-cell version, Y1 is the year of standing up the MVP. If the framework is adopted in Mode B for an existing polity, Y1 priorities are:

- Enact enabling legislation:
  - Establish Adversarial Review Body (GOV-B5).
  - Establish Future Generations Advocate (GOV-B7).
  - Strengthen Audit Office (GOV-B8) with constitutional floor.
  - Establish Citizens' Assembly (GOV-B1) with binding role on one issue (a pilot).
- Begin observability infrastructure (TAI-O):
  - Decision-log requirement extended to all major bodies.
  - Public dashboards launched.
- Begin universal-floor program:
  - Cash transfer pilot in selected regions.
  - Commons-layer baseline established for water, basic energy.
- Begin ecological-cap legislation:
  - Carbon-budget law with rising trajectory.
  - Land-value taxation pilot.
- Begin restorative-justice pilots in selected jurisdictions.
- Begin data-protection authority establishment if not present.
- Begin AI safety regulator establishment with risk-tier classification work.

## Five-Year Horizon (ART-024-Y5)

Building on Y1:

- Sortition body operating at national or top-tier scale.
- Mixed-member proportional electoral reform (if Mode B and electoral system was not already MMP).
- Constitutional reform initiated (if Mode B): convene constitutional convention; draft amendments; deliberation period.
- Universal floor extended to all members; calibration to cost of living.
- Health system universal access (extending from baseline).
- Education reform: equalized funding, capability curriculum, lifelong-learning entitlement.
- Housing reform: mixed-tenure expansion; anti-speculation measures; housing-first homelessness program.
- Energy transition: substantial renewable build-out; coal phase-out trajectory; just-transition support.
- Data rights regime fully operational.
- Algorithmic-registry operational; high-risk AI audits underway.
- Restorative-justice mainstreamed in eligible categories.
- Reserve-duration targets met for food and medicine.
- Adversarial-review body fully staffed and operating.

## Twenty-Year Horizon (ART-024-Y20)

Building on Y5:

- Constitutional reform completed (if Mode B).
- All architecture modules implemented and integrated.
- Inequality indicators in green range; mobility above OECD-leading.
- GHG emissions on net-zero trajectory; biodiversity intactness restored or stabilized.
- Healthy life expectancy improved; mental-health access universal.
- Universal education outcomes; adult learning institutionalized.
- Land-value tax mature; speculative housing market controlled.
- Cooperative sector substantial share of economy.
- Strategic-reserve and ecological-reserve programs mature.
- International coordination operational on AI, biosecurity, climate, tax.
- First multi-generational stewardship reviews conducted with future-generations advocate.
- Sortition durability demonstrated (or design adjusted in light of evidence).
- Crisis-power frameworks tested (in real or rehearsed crises) and refined.
- Member observability infrastructure operational and audited.

## Dependencies

### Legal Dependencies

- Constitutional authority for new bodies (GOV-B1 sortition, GOV-B5 adversarial review, GOV-B7 future generations).
- Strong rights protections (LAW-R) and corresponding judicial enforcement.
- Tax-law reform for new instruments (ECO-T).
- Anti-discrimination and equal-protection enforcement.
- Data-rights law (TAI-D).
- AI-regulation law (TAI-R).
- Cyber-resilience law (TAI-S).
- Ecological-cap law (ECN-B).
- Migration-and-asylum law consistent with LAW-R5 and DEF-MIG.

### Economic Dependencies

- Tax administrative capacity, including for new instruments (wealth tax, automation tax, land-value tax).
- Sovereign wealth or comparable long-horizon investment vehicle.
- Public-banking option capacity.
- Strategic-reserve buildout.
- Just-transition fund.
- Universal-floor disbursement infrastructure.

### Governance Dependencies

- Independent judiciary.
- Independent regulators.
- Audit and statistical capacity.
- Election administration independence.
- Public-service media independence.
- Civil-service depth.

### Technical Dependencies

- Open standards for public-sector software.
- Cryptographic infrastructure for observability separation.
- Civic-digital-identity infrastructure with privacy.
- Cybersecurity workforce and capability.

### Cultural and Political Dependencies

- Public legitimacy for the reform direction.
- Coalition broad enough to sustain reform across electoral cycles.
- Public-interest media ecosystem.
- Active civil society.
- Plausible adversarial-actor response anticipated.

## Mode-Specific Notes

### Mode A (Greenfield)

- Founding charter must satisfy enabling state's constitutional requirements.
- Selection-bias risk: framework's claim of generalizability requires non-self-selected cohorts at some point.
- Easier to test radical features (sortition durability, observability infrastructure, ecological caps) at small scale.

### Mode B (Reform-by-Layers)

- Most realistic for existing polities; slowest; most contested.
- Order matters: observability and adversarial-review before high-stakes redistribution; rights protections before any expansion of regulator power.
- Lessons from democratic-backsliding cases (CLM-100) reversed: strengthen rather than weaken independent institutions first.

### Mode C (Post-Crisis Reset)

- Higher legitimacy under specific conditions; greater risk of crisis-compromises.
- Truth-and-reconciliation infrastructure essential.
- International support important; not at the cost of sovereignty.

## Sequencing Principles

- **Protect first, redistribute second**: rights protections and independent bodies before expanded redistributive or regulatory power.
- **Observability before automation**: institutional decision-log and audit infrastructure operational before AI-assisted public-sector tools deployed.
- **Floor before ceilings**: universal-floor and commons before stricter ceilings on consumption and concentration.
- **Pilot before scale**: any high-risk feature piloted at sub-national scale before national rollout.
- **Communicate before regulating**: explain rationale and tradeoffs publicly; build coalition; show evidence.
- **Compensate before transforming**: just-transition before policies that displace livelihoods.
- **Hardware before software**: physical reserves, redundancy, and infrastructure before procedural reform that depends on them.

## Exit and Rollback Mechanisms

The framework explicitly acknowledges that any specific implementation may fail. Exit and rollback are constitutional features, not failures.

- **Sunset clauses** on all major regulations (default 7 years for executive regulations; longer for statutes).
- **Constitutional-convention review** every 20-25 years (TZ-003) provides scheduled review opportunities.
- **Module-update procedure** in the iteration protocol (`canon/iteration-protocol.md`) permits revising individual modules without rewriting the whole framework.
- **Reversion clauses**: any reform may be repealed by the same procedure that enacted it; reversion to pre-reform state is possible, with transitional protections for those who relied on the reform.
- **Exit rights for cells and regions**: under defined conditions, sub-units may leave the polity (constitutional and international-law processes required); the framework does not coerce membership.
- **Conscientious-objector accommodation**: individual members who cannot in conscience participate in specific reforms have accommodation options where they do not conflict with universal rights.

## Risks of the Implementation Itself

- Reform fatigue: too much change at once collapses public support.
- Adversarial response: opponents act before reforms consolidate; capture-resistance must be operational early.
- Just-transition under-funded: workers harmed by reform mobilize against it.
- Implementation lag: laws pass faster than capacity is built; reform fails through delivery.
- International isolation: trading partners or adversaries respond to reform.
- Self-overreach: reformers take wins as license to skip procedure; legitimacy erodes.

Each risk is addressed in the adoption playbook (ART-025) and governance-migration plan (ART-026).

## Indicators of Successful Adoption

- Indicator-panel improvement on schedule.
- Reforms hold across at least two electoral or generational cycles.
- Adversarial-review body active and respected.
- Public deliberation working; sortition bodies functioning.
- Civil-society engagement high.
- Trust in institutions rising.
- Capture-resistance audits clean.

## Open Issues

- Costing of the full transition is highly context-dependent; specific budgets must be built per polity.
- Coalition politics of reform is contingent and difficult to specify abstractly.
- The framework's claim of universality must be tested with non-self-selected cohorts; until then, scale-up generalizability is uncertain.
- International-cooperation dependencies (tax, AI, climate, biosecurity) are not under unilateral control; framework partially depends on partners.
- Post-crisis (Mode C) implementations are most fragile; framework requires special transition guidance not fully specified here.
