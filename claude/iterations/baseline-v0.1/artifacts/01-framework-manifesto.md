# Framework Manifesto

Artifact ID: ART-002

Status: draft
Change type: new
Owner module: all

## What this framework is

This framework is a design specification for a human society that is meant to last: through ecological constraint, through technological disruption, through the recurring dance of demagogues, through pandemics and wars and the slower failures that finish off institutions. It is not a utopia. It does not assume that people are good, that leaders are wise, that resources are unlimited, or that consensus can be permanent.

It assumes the opposite. It assumes that power concentrates, that institutions decay, that crises come in waves, that adversaries learn, that ecological limits are real, and that the only durable response is structural: institutions that work without requiring extraordinary virtue from those who staff them, and that correct themselves when they fail.

## What it is not

It is not a model for "the best society." It is a model for *a society that survives long enough to keep getting better*. The framework treats progress as something measured (in capability, wellbeing, justice, resilience, ecological balance) and earned (through institutional work). The framework refuses single-metric optimization. It refuses to assume that markets, planners, AIs, or charismatic leaders will solve human problems automatically.

It is not a blueprint imposable on existing societies wholesale. The implementation roadmap (ART-024) recognizes that reform takes decades and runs through political weather it cannot fully control. The framework is offered as a coherent target. Whether and how to approach it is a matter for the people doing the approaching.

## The nineteen values

The framework rests on a value constitution of nineteen values (ART-008). They are not equally weighted in every case; tradeoffs are explicit, not silently resolved. The values are:

human dignity and rights; health by design; ecological boundaries; resilience as architecture; legitimate and consent-based governance; capture-resistant governance; competence with accountability; justice and rule of law; science-guided policy; pluralism and freedom of conscience; education as foundation; mental health as civic infrastructure; self-sufficiency and chosen interdependence; sustainable progress beyond GDP; privacy and data rights; intergenerational stewardship; care and social reproduction; continuous self-correction; internal observability and secure boundaries.

Each value has indicators. Each value has known conflicts with one or more others. Each conflict has a documented resolution procedure. The framework does not pretend the values cohere automatically; it does the work of saying who bears the cost when they do not.

## The architecture, briefly

Governance is multi-tier and multi-modal. Local cells of around 5,000-15,000 members do most everyday work; regional federations coordinate; a national tier handles defense, monetary policy, constitutional review, and framework-wide standards. At the national level, eleven bodies exist: a sortition citizens' assembly, an elected representative assembly, a constitutional court, a collective executive, a standing adversarial-review body, a sortition chamber with confirmatory powers, a future-generations advocate, an audit office, an ombuds network, independent regulators, and a federal council of regions.

No single body can sustain a captured outcome against the others. Rotation, mixed selection, term limits, transparent reasoning, public decision logs, and an adversarial-review body with subpoena power do the structural work. Emergency powers exist but expire automatically and require supermajority renewal; non-derogable rights hold throughout.

The economy has five layers: a commons layer (water, basic energy, primary health, primary education, basic transit, basic digital connectivity); a public-provisioning layer (full healthcare, full education, eldercare, childcare); a regulated market layer for most goods and services; a discretionary market for luxuries; and structurally separate regimes for land, finance, and ecological commons. A universal cash floor on top of the commons severs survival from labor coercion. Wealth and income concentration are constrained by progressive taxation, pay-ratio rules, antitrust, and land-value taxation. Care work and unpaid labor are recognized economically.

Law protects civil, political, economic, social, cultural, group, and digital rights, with non-derogable cores that hold in emergency. Public safety is multi-modal: civilian responders for non-violent calls, trained police for violent incidents, restorative-justice pathways for eligible offenses, prison reserved for cases where it is genuinely necessary. Anti-corruption mechanisms are aggressive.

Health is universal, anchored in primary care, trauma-informed by default, with mental health treated as civic infrastructure on parity with physical health. Public health surveillance is narrow, time-limited, separated from law enforcement.

Education is universal from early childhood through tertiary, with lifelong-learning entitlements. The curriculum is broad: literacy and numeracy, civic and statistical literacy, practical self-sufficiency, emotional and relational capabilities, ecological literacy, arts, physical capability. Tracking is deferred. Funding is equalized; need-weighted.

Community is organized around cells with functional roles, conflict-resolution capacity, care infrastructure, and pluralism protections. Belonging without coercion is the goal.

Ecology and infrastructure operate under constitutional ceilings on greenhouse gases, water, biodiversity, soil, and pollution. Land cannot be unconditionally captured as private rent. Housing is mixed-tenure with anti-speculation rules. Energy transitions away from fossil fuels on a defined trajectory. Reserve durations for food, energy, and medicine are funded and rehearsed.

Technology and AI are governed under a four-tier risk classification. High-impact algorithmic decisions require human accountability, audit, and recourse. Mass surveillance, social credit scoring, autonomous lethal decisions, and a defined list of other uses are prohibited. Strong encryption is protected; lawful access is targeted, not back-doored. Member observability of institutions is built on cryptographically separated infrastructure that does not become surveillance of people.

Defense is credible but restrained. Civil defense and resilience infrastructure are real. Migration is rights-respecting with accessible naturalization. External boundaries (physical, cyber, informational, economic, biosecurity, knowledge) are explicitly maintained and audited annually. Emergency powers are sharply limited.

## How it corrects itself

The framework includes its own critique. A standing adversarial-review body is funded to disagree, audit, and publish. Decisions are logged. Assumptions are registered. Contradictions are named. Risks are scored. Stress scenarios are specified. An adversarial review of the framework itself (ART-031) records what each perspective would damage in the design.

The iteration protocol (`canon/iteration-protocol.md`) supports module updates, simulation updates, implementation updates, and full re-research. Constitutional amendment is hard, but possible; periodic review every 20-25 years is required. Sunset clauses make laws expire absent reauthorization.

## What it requires from those who adopt it

Capacity. Coalition. Patience.

Capacity: independent judiciaries, capable administrations, public-service media, civil-society depth, technical workforce. Where capacity is missing, the first phase of adoption is building it.

Coalition: a stable supermajority for foundational protections; working majorities for ordinary reforms; sustained legitimacy across electoral cycles. Adoption that races ahead of legitimacy will be reversed.

Patience: the architecture is meant to function across generations. The framework's twenty-year horizon (ART-024-Y20) is itself a beginning, not a completion. Behavior at scale changes slowly. Institutions mature over decades. The framework asks for the kind of patience that built cathedrals and watershed councils.

## What it admits

The framework is honest about its uncertainties. Forty assumptions in `assumption-register.md` are unverified. Twenty-four contradictions in `contradiction-register.md` are documented; most are resolved with explicit tradeoffs, several remain open. Forty-eight risks in `risk-register.md` are scored with their residual exposure. Many empirical claims (in `evidence-matrix.md`) are contested or theoretical; design choices that depend on them are flagged.

The framework's most load-bearing element is the adversarial-review body. If it fails, much of the capture-resistance collapses. The framework's most ambitious bet is that hard ecological caps can be politically maintained across electoral cycles in a society that depends on the systems those caps constrain. The framework's most contested feature is sortition at scale; its durability is not yet demonstrated.

The framework assumes substantial international cooperation on tax, climate, biosecurity, and AI. Where that cooperation fails, several pillars are under stress.

The framework's behavioral research base skews WEIRD; cross-cultural variation may invalidate specific design choices.

## What it asks

That whoever uses this framework — to design a community, reform a state, evaluate a proposal, criticize a faction, or imagine a future — uses it adversarially. Look for what it gets wrong. Test it against your specific context. Apply it where it fits and amend it where it does not.

The framework's only durable claim is that the work of self-correction is itself a value, and that institutions which take that work seriously have a chance.

## Closing

A civilization is not a building. It is a set of practices that keep getting redone, by people who keep getting older and being replaced, in a physical world that keeps changing. This framework is an attempt to specify those practices in a way that makes the redoing easier, the corrections faster, and the failures more recoverable.

It will be wrong about many things. It is designed to be revised. Begin.
