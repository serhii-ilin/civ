# Defense and External Relations

Artifact ID: ART-017
Iteration: baseline-v0.1
Status: draft
Change type: new
Owner module: MOD-DEF
Depends on: ART-008, ART-009, ART-011

## Purpose

Specify how the framework defends itself, conducts foreign relations, manages migration, trades, and controls external boundaries while preserving values, legitimacy, and internal freedoms. Bound by `value-constitution.md` (ART-008), `governance-system.md` (ART-009), and `law-and-justice-system.md` (ART-011).

The framework rejects two poles: militarized society where defense displaces civic life, and naive openness where adversaries can dismantle institutions through coercion, information, capital, or migration shock. The design seeks credible defense, restrained foreign policy, principled migration, and explicit external-boundary discipline.

## Threat Model (DEF-T)

The framework plans for the following categories. Probability and intensity depend on geography; the system is modular.

### DEF-T1 Conventional Military

- State or coalition military attack on territory, populations, or infrastructure.
- Probability: variable, materially shaped by geography and alliance posture.

### DEF-T2 Coercion Short of War

- Economic coercion, energy or trade blackmail, sanctions weaponization, supply-chain manipulation, financial-system attacks.

### DEF-T3 Information Warfare

- Influence operations, disinformation, electoral interference, deepfake operations, narrative weaponization.

### DEF-T4 Cyber Attack

- State and non-state attacks on critical infrastructure, public-sector systems, supply chain (RSK-008).

### DEF-T5 Hybrid Operations

- Combined kinetic, cyber, information, economic, and sabotage operations; often through proxies.

### DEF-T6 Terrorism and Asymmetric

- Non-state armed actors; lone actors; insider attacks.

### DEF-T7 Biological and Chemical

- State or non-state release of biological or chemical agents (RSK-007 overlap).

### DEF-T8 Climate-Driven Pressure

- Climate-driven migration, resource competition, instability in neighboring states (RSK-006, RSK-012).

### DEF-T9 AI-Enabled Threats

- AI-enabled cyber attacks, autonomous-weapon proliferation, AI-enabled mass-manipulation (RSK-042, TAI-R2 prohibitions).

### DEF-T10 Domestic Insurrection or Coup

- Internal armed challenge to constitutional order; foreign-backed factionalism (RSK-005, RSK-030).

## Military Architecture (DEF-M)

### DEF-M1 Doctrine

- Defensive by intent; credible deterrence by capability.
- Proportionality and discrimination as binding constraints.
- Civilian control of military absolute (LAW-R5, GOV-D4).
- No first use of nuclear weapons (if applicable); commitment to disarmament with reciprocity.

### DEF-M2 Force Structure

- Combined arms forces calibrated to threat environment.
- Reserve and conscript elements where appropriate; militia-style structures (Swiss-like, BMK-004) for some functions.
- Specialized capabilities (cyber, special operations, intelligence) integrated.

### DEF-M3 Civil-Military Boundary

- Military operations on home territory limited to disaster response, civil defense, and narrow specified circumstances.
- Posse comitatus-style principle: military not deployed in domestic law-enforcement role except under explicit constitutional authority with strict limits.
- See DEF-D for civil-military separation in crisis.

### DEF-M4 Personnel

- Voluntary service with conscription reserved as constitutional possibility under specified threat.
- Rights of service members preserved (LAW-R1 within necessary discipline limits); independent military ombuds.
- Mental-health support for service members and veterans.
- Diversity of background actively recruited.

### DEF-M5 Procurement and Industry

- Defense procurement subject to GOV-O transparency to extent compatible with operational security.
- Defense industrial base supported for resilience; export controls.
- Indigenous capability in defined critical areas.

### DEF-M6 Use of Force

- Decisions to use force require GOV-D4 process.
- Emergency self-defense permitted; reporting to GOV-B2 within 48 hours.
- Targeting decisions reviewed; war-crimes accountability.
- No autonomous lethal-decision systems (TAI-R2).

### DEF-M7 Veterans

- Comprehensive veterans' care: physical, mental, vocational, housing.
- Civic reintegration.

## Civil Defense (DEF-D)

### DEF-D1 Standing Civil Defense

- Civil-defense organization with local presence and trained volunteers.
- Annual drills.
- Equipment caches at community level.
- Reflects BMK-037 (Cuba, Japan, NZ) and lessons from BMK-004.

### DEF-D2 Continuity Plans

- National, regional, and local continuity-of-government plans, rehearsed.
- Continuity of essential services (health, water, food, communications).
- Public communications during crisis (HEA-P3 coordination).

### DEF-D3 Population Preparedness

- Public education on preparedness (EDU-C3).
- Community-level preparedness audits.
- Equipment standards for households where appropriate.

### DEF-D4 Critical-Infrastructure Resilience

- Per ECN-IC.
- Cyber resilience per TAI-S.

### DEF-D5 Emergency Reserves

- Food, water, medicine, energy, financial.
- Reserve-duration targets per VAL-004.

### DEF-D6 Mass-Casualty and Disaster Response

- Surge capacity in health system (HEA-P3).
- Search-and-rescue, fire, hazardous-materials response.
- Mutual aid agreements regionally and internationally.

## Foreign Policy (DEF-F)

### DEF-F1 Principles

- Diplomatic engagement as default.
- Respect for international law and treaty obligations.
- Human rights as foreign-policy consideration, not weapon of selective interest.
- Mutual benefit in economic relations; reciprocity in coercive measures.

### DEF-F2 Alliances and Coalitions

- Alliances calibrated to threat environment; long-term partnerships protected.
- Coalitions of the willing on specific issues (climate, biosecurity, AI governance).
- Permanent neutrality option where geographic and historical conditions support it (BMK-004 reference).

### DEF-F3 International Institutions

- Active participation in international bodies (UN, regional organizations, sectoral bodies).
- Investment in international rule-making on emerging issues (AI, climate, biosecurity, cyber).
- Compliance with binding international law.

### DEF-F4 Trade Policy

- Open trade as default; managed trade in strategic categories (ECO-MK4).
- Labor, environmental, and human-rights standards embedded in trade agreements.
- Tariff and non-tariff instruments used carefully; transparency on use.

### DEF-F5 Development Cooperation

- Aid and partnership programs with poorer countries.
- Climate-finance commitments.
- Refugee and humanitarian response.

### DEF-F6 Sanctions

- Targeted sanctions over broad sanctions where possible.
- Human-rights and humanitarian-impact assessment required before imposition.
- Periodic review; sunset clauses.
- Independent civil-society and academic oversight of sanctions effects.

### DEF-F7 Intelligence

- Foreign intelligence service operationally separate from domestic security.
- Oversight by GOV-B5 with access to classified materials.
- Strict prohibitions on intelligence activity against own members on home territory absent specific judicial authorization.
- Prohibition on torture, extrajudicial killing, secret detention.

### DEF-F8 Disarmament and Non-Proliferation

- Active participation in non-proliferation regimes (nuclear, biological, chemical, conventional, autonomous-weapons, cyber).
- Verification cooperation.

## External Boundaries (DEF-B)

The framework explicitly maintains external boundaries across multiple domains, audited for security and for non-abusiveness.

### DEF-B1 Physical Boundary

- Territorial integrity protected.
- Border management is non-militarized at peace; police-style with right protections (LAW-P6).
- Smuggling and trafficking interdicted under rule of law.

### DEF-B2 Cyber Boundary

- Defensive cybersecurity (TAI-S).
- Identification of foreign-state cyber action with attribution discipline.
- Coordination with allies.

### DEF-B3 Informational Boundary

- Foreign-state influence operations on platforms tracked and disclosed.
- Foreign-funded political activity disclosed; foreign electoral contributions prohibited (GOV-A4).
- Public-service media as resilient information backbone.

### DEF-B4 Economic Boundary

- Foreign-direct-investment screening in strategic sectors.
- Beneficial-ownership transparency.
- Anti-money-laundering enforcement.
- Capital-flow monitoring during stress without imposing harm on member savers.

### DEF-B5 Migration Boundary

- See DEF-MIG below.

### DEF-B6 Biosecurity Boundary

- Border-health surveillance.
- Trade-related agricultural-biosecurity controls.
- Coordination with international health regulations.

### DEF-B7 Knowledge Boundary

- Sensitive-knowledge transfer controls (dual-use technology, weapons-related research).
- Visiting-scholar and researcher visa screening proportional to risk.
- Academic openness preserved as default.

### DEF-B8 Boundary Audit

- All external boundaries audited annually by GOV-B5.
- Audits assess both security effectiveness and rights compliance.
- Audit findings public to extent compatible with operational security.

## Migration and Asylum (DEF-MIG)

### DEF-MIG1 Asylum

- Asylum rights honored per refugee convention and constitutional rights.
- Fair, prompt, dignified process; legal representation; appeal.
- Reception conditions humane; detention not the default.
- Non-refoulement non-derogable.

### DEF-MIG2 Immigration

- Lawful immigration channels for family, work, study, humanitarian, and integration paths.
- Annual planning with regional input.
- Skills-based and family-based pathways balanced.

### DEF-MIG3 Integration

- Reception services in receiving cells (CUL-P6).
- Language instruction free for new arrivals.
- Civic-orientation programs.
- Anti-discrimination enforcement (LAW-R1, CUL-P3).
- Permanent-residency and naturalization paths after qualifying period.

### DEF-MIG4 Citizenship

- Birthright and qualified-naturalization paths.
- Naturalization criteria: residency, civic knowledge, language proficiency at modest threshold, no significant criminal record.
- Equal rights for naturalized citizens.
- Dual citizenship permitted unless specific reciprocity issues.

### DEF-MIG5 Climate Migration

- Anticipation and reception planning (ECN-CA3).
- Cooperation with sending regions and international coordination.
- Climate-displaced persons recognized within evolving international framework.

### DEF-MIG6 Stateless Persons

- Identification and pathway to legal status.
- No member of the polity allowed to fall into statelessness through state action.

### DEF-MIG7 Returns

- Returns of failed asylum-seekers conducted with rights protection.
- Cooperation with international monitors.
- No collective expulsion.

## Crisis Powers and Constraints (DEF-C)

Bound by GOV-D5 and LAW-E.

### DEF-C1 Declaration

- War declaration: 2/3 of GOV-B2, 3/5 of GOV-B6.
- State of emergency for non-war crises per GOV-D5.
- Public reasoning, reporting, sunset.

### DEF-C2 Conduct in War

- International humanitarian law binding.
- Geneva Conventions and Additional Protocols.
- War-crimes accountability through national and international mechanisms.
- Treatment of prisoners and protected persons under conventions.

### DEF-C3 Civil-Liberties Floor

- Non-derogable rights (LAW-R5) hold in war and emergency.
- Courts continue to operate; habeas corpus available.
- Press and information freedoms preserved with narrow operational-security limits.

### DEF-C4 End of Crisis

- Auto-expiry of crisis powers; reauthorization requirements (GOV-D5).
- Post-crisis review by GOV-B5 mandatory.
- Reparations and accountability for misuse.

## Indicators (DEF-I)

- Threat-environment indicators.
- Military readiness, training, equipment, personnel wellbeing.
- Civil-defense drill outcomes.
- Critical-infrastructure resilience.
- External-boundary audits: security and rights.
- Migration: asylum processing time, integration outcomes, return-process compliance.
- Foreign-policy: treaty compliance, alliance commitments, sanctions impact, development-aid effectiveness.
- Intelligence: oversight reports, complaint findings.
- War, conflict, and crisis: combat operations, casualty counts, accountability proceedings.
- Veterans: physical and mental-health outcomes, housing, employment.
- Information environment: foreign-influence operations detected and disclosed.

## Open Issues

- Doctrine of "no first use" interacts with deterrence credibility; geographic context shapes feasibility.
- Conscription rules (DEF-M4) are contested; the framework defers to constitutional process and threat environment.
- Sanctions effectiveness (DEF-F6) is empirically mixed; the framework leans toward targeted measures.
- AI-enabled threats (DEF-T9) are fast-evolving; international coordination required.
- Climate-migration framework (DEF-MIG5) is still developing internationally; the framework anticipates engagement.
- Intelligence-and-civil-liberties balance (DEF-F7) is the largest tension here; the framework leans toward narrow authorization and strong oversight.
- Posse-comitatus-style military-on-home-territory rules (DEF-M3) are politically sensitive; the framework defaults conservative.
- Civil-defense and militia structures (DEF-M2) interact with civilian gun-control policy; the framework requires regulated structure, not generalized armament.
