# Education System

Artifact ID: ART-013
Iteration: baseline-v0.1
Status: draft
Change type: new
Owner module: MOD-EDU
Depends on: ART-008, ART-012

## Purpose

Specify the education system that produces resilient, capable, ethical, emotionally intelligent, practically skilled, and civically competent people across their lifespan. Bound by `value-constitution.md` (ART-008), `health-and-mental-health-system.md` (ART-012), and behavioral foundations in ART-007.

The design favors broad capability over narrow credentialism, intrinsic motivation over coercive testing, equitable resourcing over school-level competition, and lifelong learning over one-shot schooling.

## Phases (EDU-P)

### EDU-P1 Early Childhood (0-6)

- Universal access to high-quality childcare and early-childhood education from age 1 (or earlier per family).
- Parental leave (paid) protected before age 1 to support attachment (B-008, CLM-063).
- Home-visiting programs for vulnerable families.
- Curriculum focused on play, language, motor, social-emotional, and physical development. Not academic acceleration.
- Caregiver-to-child ratios at evidence-based levels.
- Funding: public; means-free for the universal floor.

### EDU-P2 Primary (6-12)

- Universal public primary; equitably resourced regardless of neighborhood (national funding equalization).
- Curriculum: literacy, numeracy, science literacy, civics, arts, physical education, ecological literacy, basic digital literacy, social-emotional learning, second language.
- Pedagogy: developmentally appropriate; intrinsic-motivation aware (CLM-051); recess and outdoor time protected.
- Class sizes: small enough for relational teaching.

### EDU-P3 Lower Secondary (12-16)

- Universal; broad common curriculum.
- Introduction to practical skills (food preparation, basic finance, basic repair, digital literacy, civic participation, first aid, environmental stewardship).
- Pathways still flexible; tracking deferred.

### EDU-P4 Upper Secondary and Equivalents (16-19)

- Multiple aligned pathways: academic, technical-vocational, mixed.
- All pathways carry the framework's common civic-and-capability curriculum.
- Industry-aligned vocational programs are funded and high status, not residual.
- Apprenticeships with workplace and classroom components.

### EDU-P5 Tertiary (post-secondary)

- Universities, technical institutes, professional schools, conservatories.
- Need-blind admissions; need-based aid; tuition-free or low-cost public tertiary education.
- Adult learners welcomed and supported.

### EDU-P6 Lifelong Learning

- Reskilling and continuing-education entitlements: every adult has a publicly funded learning account, usable across approved programs.
- Sabbatical entitlements for workers in long tenure for retraining or refreshing.
- Community-based learning hubs in every locality.

## Core Curriculum (EDU-C)

The framework's curriculum is built around capabilities every adult should reliably have, plus pathway-specific specialization. Capabilities:

### EDU-C1 Cognitive Capabilities

- Literacy in dominant language(s) of polity; ideally second-language proficiency.
- Numeracy through introductory statistics.
- Critical thinking, source evaluation, and statistical reasoning.
- Scientific literacy and the nature of evidence.
- Computational literacy and digital fluency.
- Media literacy, including algorithmic-environment awareness.

### EDU-C2 Civic Capabilities

- Constitutional literacy, including value-constitution and rights.
- Procedural literacy: how decisions are made, how to participate, how to file complaints, how to vote, how to challenge errors.
- Comparative civic-systems awareness.
- Practice in deliberation and conflict resolution.

### EDU-C3 Practical Self-Sufficiency

- Food: planning, cooking, basic nutrition.
- Finance: budgeting, contracts, taxes, public benefits.
- Health: basic first aid, mental-health literacy, sexual and reproductive health, substance-use literacy.
- Repair and maintenance: home, basic tools, basic electronics.
- Emergency readiness: disaster, household, community.
- Digital systems: cybersecurity hygiene, data rights, productive use.
- Local governance: how to engage with local cell, where to find local resources.

### EDU-C4 Relational and Emotional Capabilities

- Emotional literacy and regulation.
- Conflict resolution and communication.
- Healthy relationships (including consent, equality, boundaries).
- Trauma awareness (basic, age-appropriate).
- Cooperation in teams.

### EDU-C5 Ecological and Place-Based

- Ecological literacy: ecosystems, planetary boundaries, climate.
- Local-place knowledge: watershed, biodiversity, history, neighbors.
- Stewardship practices.

### EDU-C6 Arts and Cultural

- Music, visual art, drama, literature, dance.
- Cultural heritage of polity and of resident communities.
- Aesthetic and creative practice.

### EDU-C7 Physical and Outdoor

- Movement education, lifelong activity habits.
- Outdoor experience, including overnight wilderness experience for adolescents where geographically feasible.

### EDU-C8 Specialization (pathway-specific)

- Academic-track depth in chosen subject areas.
- Vocational-track depth in chosen trade or technical field.
- Cross-pathway electives.

## Pedagogy and Assessment (EDU-A)

### EDU-A1 Pedagogical Defaults

- Active learning, inquiry, project-based learning where appropriate; explicit instruction for foundational skills.
- Differentiation for varied learners.
- Trauma-informed default (B-009; mental-health-system coordination per ART-012).
- Autonomy-supportive practice (CLM-051).

### EDU-A2 Assessment

- Formative assessment to support learning, frequent and low-stakes.
- Summative assessment for credentials, moderated for fairness.
- Capability assessments (practical, oral, project, written) alongside written tests.
- No high-stakes single-event determination of life pathway.
- Bias auditing of all standardized assessments.

### EDU-A3 Credentials

- Credentials reflect demonstrated capability, not just exam performance.
- Multiple pathways carry credential parity (no second-class vocational tag).
- Recognition of prior learning (RPL) processes for adults.

### EDU-A4 Standardized Testing

- Sampled, low-stakes national assessment for system monitoring (similar in spirit to PISA, NAEP).
- High-stakes individual standardized testing minimized; not the sole gateway to opportunity.

## Teachers and Educators (EDU-T)

### EDU-T1 Selection

- Selective teacher education; respected profession.
- Pay competitive with comparable professional careers.
- Diversity of background actively pursued.

### EDU-T2 Education and Development

- Robust initial teacher education with significant practicum.
- Mentoring for first years.
- Continuing professional development as part of work hours.

### EDU-T3 Working Conditions

- Class size manageable.
- Non-instructional time for planning, collaboration, family contact.
- Support staff (special education, counseling, school nurses, social workers, librarians).

### EDU-T4 Assessment and Removal

- Teacher evaluation includes multiple measures: observation, student-outcome trends, peer review.
- Underperformance addressed through support; persistent failure with cause leads to removal.
- No single test-score-based dismissal.

## Equity (EDU-E)

### EDU-E1 Resource Equalization

- School funding equalized across regions and within regions; need-weighted (higher funding for higher-need populations).
- Capital investment for facility quality equity.

### EDU-E2 Targeted Supports

- Early intervention for children at risk.
- Free meals; meals of nutritional quality (HEA-P5).
- Free transport.
- Special-education services as right.
- Multilingual support for emergent bilinguals.

### EDU-E3 School Choice

- Public schools as default and well-resourced.
- Private schools permitted, regulated to prevent civil-rights violations; cannot exempt from anti-discrimination law; cannot receive public funds if they discriminate.
- Charter and alternative public schools where they expand pluralism without producing stratification.
- Watchfulness against school-choice patterns that increase segregation (RSK-028).

### EDU-E4 Indigenous and Minority-Language Education

- Indigenous-language-medium schools where communities choose.
- Bilingual and minority-language programs.
- Cultural curriculum developed with communities.

### EDU-E5 Disability Inclusion

- Default inclusion in mainstream settings with specialized supports.
- Special programs where individually justified.

### EDU-E6 Boys and Girls

- Co-educational default with attention to gender-equity outcomes.
- Anti-harassment policies enforced.

### EDU-E7 Anti-Tracking

- Tracking deferred to upper secondary (EDU-P4).
- Movement between tracks possible.
- Early tracking shown to reproduce class structure (CLM-110, RSK-028); avoided.

## Curriculum Governance (EDU-G)

- National standards on core curriculum (EDU-C1-C7).
- Local adaptation on EDU-C5 (place-based) and within other domains.
- Curriculum reviewed every 7 years by mixed expert and citizen body.
- Textbook and material approval transparent; bias audits.
- Religion and life-stance education: comparative and historical in public schools; faith-formation by families and religious bodies on their own time and resources; private religious schools regulated against discrimination and indoctrination harmful to children.

## Higher Education (EDU-H)

### EDU-H1 Mission

- Teaching, research, public service.
- Free inquiry protected.

### EDU-H2 Funding

- Block-grant funding for teaching and basic research.
- Competitive grants for project research.
- Public-good-aligned mission for endowment investment.

### EDU-H3 Admissions and Access

- Need-blind admissions; need-based aid covers full cost.
- Outreach to under-represented groups.
- Adult-learner pathways.

### EDU-H4 Academic Freedom

- Tenure protected for research and teaching freedom.
- Institutional autonomy protected; subject to public accountability.

### EDU-H5 Research Integrity

- Replication, pre-registration, open access (CLM-027 broader principle on transparency).
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure.
- Misconduct procedures with due process.

### EDU-H6 Privatization Limits

- Universities cannot transfer their core public mission to private parties.
- Industry partnerships transparent.

## Adult Education and Reskilling (EDU-R)

- Lifelong learning entitlement per EDU-P6.
- Reskilling funded through automation and externality taxes (ECO-T4, ECO-T5).
- Counseling and assessment services to map career transitions.
- Modular credentials stack into degree-equivalents over time.

## Digital Infrastructure for Education (EDU-D)

- Public digital learning platform with high-quality materials at all levels.
- Open educational resources by default for publicly funded curriculum.
- Member privacy protected; no commercial advertising to students.
- Algorithmic tools used in education subject to ART-016.

## Indicators (EDU-I)

- Literacy, numeracy, scientific literacy, civic literacy by cohort.
- Capability assessments (multi-modal) by cohort.
- Equity gaps by region, socio-economic status, language, ethnicity, disability.
- Retention, completion, transitions across phases.
- Tertiary participation and completion.
- Adult-learning participation.
- Teacher retention, satisfaction, burnout.
- Inter-generational mobility (educational and economic) (CLM-110).

## Open Issues

- The framework's capability-broad approach may strain class-time; tradeoffs with depth of subject expertise need careful curriculum design.
- The boundary between civic education and ideological indoctrination is contested; the framework defaults to pluralism plus shared constitutional literacy.
- Assessment without high-stakes individual standardized testing risks credential signaling problems; the framework leans on multi-modal portfolio plus capability assessment.
- Religious-school regulation (EDU-G last paragraph) balances pluralism and child rights; the framework adopts a narrow harm test plus anti-discrimination.
- Higher-education funding model is politically contested; the framework adopts tuition-free or low-cost public tertiary, which has transition costs.
- AI in classrooms (writing, tutoring) is fast-evolving; framework regulation must adapt (ART-016).
