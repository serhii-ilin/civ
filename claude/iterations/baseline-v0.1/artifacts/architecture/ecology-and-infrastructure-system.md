## Purpose

Specify the ecological, spatial, and infrastructure foundations. Bound by `value-constitution.md` (ART-008), `economic-system.md` (ART-010), and the ecological evidence in ART-004.

Three principles bind the design: (i) hard ecological caps in law and budget; (ii) redundant local capacity for essentials; (iii) land, water, energy, food, and infrastructure managed across multi-generational horizons rather than electoral cycles.

## Ecological Boundaries (ECN-B)

### ECN-B1 Constitutional Ceilings

- Greenhouse gas budget set in law, declining trajectory aligned with science-based 1.5 to 2 degrees Celsius pathways (CLM-072).
- Watershed-level water-withdrawal caps, set below sustainable yield.
- Biodiversity-protection floors: protected-area share and ecological connectivity targets.
- Soil-quality floors: net-zero soil degradation by date X.
- Pollution caps (air, water, soil, noise, light).
- Material-footprint trajectory: declining toward sustainable levels.

### ECN-B2 Boundary-Setting Process

- Boundaries set by independent ecological-science body (a GOV-B10 regulator), confirmed by GOV-B2 legislation, reviewed every 5 years.
- Boundaries are binding; budgets, regulations, and approvals must comply.

### ECN-B3 Allocation Mechanisms

- Where a binding cap creates scarcity (carbon, water, fish), allocations use a mix of:
  - Caps with auction and per-citizen dividend (variant of cap-and-dividend).
  - Standards (e.g., emissions standards for sectors).
  - Bans for high-harm activities.
  - Conservation set-asides.
- See ECO-T4.

### ECN-B4 Polluter-Pays and Precaution

- Polluter-pays principle codified.
- Precautionary principle for novel risks (genetic, geoengineering, novel chemicals, nanomaterials).

### ECN-B5 Ecocide and Environmental Crime

- Recognized as criminal offense per LAW-CJ6.
- Restitution to ecological commons; restoration requirements.

## Land and Spatial Planning (ECN-L)

### ECN-L1 Land Tenure

- Land cannot be unconditionally owned in a way that captures unearned community-generated value (ECO-L5).
- Mixed tenure forms: private ownership (with land-value tax), public ownership, community land trusts, indigenous title, common lands.
- Inheritance limits on landholdings.

### ECN-L2 Spatial Planning

- Multi-tier land-use planning: national strategic plan; regional plans; local-cell plans.
- Plans set densities, mix of uses, ecological-protection zones, agricultural-protection zones, infrastructure corridors.
- Plans are public, deliberated, and reviewed every 7 years.

### ECN-L3 Urbanism for Wellbeing

- Walkable, mixed-use neighborhoods with 15-minute access to daily needs.
- Public transit anchored before housing growth.
- Active mobility infrastructure (cycling, walking).
- Public space at minimum percentage of urban land.
- Affordable housing required at percentage in every development.

### ECN-L4 Agricultural and Conservation Land

- Agricultural-protection zones with rights of farming use only.
- Conservation-protection zones permanently protected.
- Active stewardship payments where land is held for ecological function.

### ECN-L5 Indigenous Land

- Recognition of indigenous title; participatory governance for indigenous-managed land; free, prior, and informed consent for impacting activity.

### ECN-L6 Anti-Speculation Measures

- Vacant-land tax.
- Vacancy tax on residential property.
- Limits on absentee ownership for primary-residence stock.
- Real-estate transaction transparency, including beneficial-ownership disclosure.

## Housing (ECN-H)

### ECN-H1 Housing as Right

- Adequate housing is a right (LAW-R2.3).
- The state guarantees an offer of dignified housing to every member.

### ECN-H2 Mixed Tenure

- Owner-occupation (with land-value tax) — typically 40-55% of stock.
- Social housing — typically 20-35% of stock.
- Cooperative housing — typically 5-15% of stock.
- Regulated private rental — typically 15-30% of stock.

Percentages illustrative; calibrated regionally.

### ECN-H3 Quality Standards

- Minimum-quality standards (space, sanitation, ventilation, energy efficiency, accessibility).
- Substandard housing addressed by remediation or replacement, not eviction.

### ECN-H4 Anti-Displacement

- Tenant protections: just-cause eviction, rent regulation, anti-discrimination, right to representation.
- Relocation assistance where redevelopment necessary.

### ECN-H5 Energy and Climate Resilience

- All new construction net-zero or net-positive.
- Existing-stock retrofit programs at scale; just-transition for landlords and tenants.
- Climate-resilient design (heat, flood, fire).

### ECN-H6 Homelessness

- Housing-first approach: stable housing as precondition, not reward for compliance.
- Wraparound services available, not mandatory for housing access.
- Eliminating chronic homelessness as policy target.

## Water (ECN-W)

### ECN-W1 Right to Water

- Basic water for personal and household use is a right (LAW-R2.4).
- Volume above the basic floor is priced to encourage conservation; pricing structures protect the basic floor.

### ECN-W2 Watershed Governance

- Watersheds are primary water-governance units.
- Watershed councils with all stakeholders represented.
- Withdrawal caps below sustainable yield.

### ECN-W3 Infrastructure

- Universal access to safe drinking water.
- Universal sanitation.
- Wastewater treatment to ecological standards.
- Stormwater management for resilience and water quality.
- Reuse and recycling at scale where feasible.

### ECN-W4 Quality Protection

- Source-water protection.
- Industrial and agricultural pollution constrained.
- Drinking-water quality monitoring publicly reported.

### ECN-W5 Climate Adaptation

- Drought response plans.
- Flood-protection infrastructure and risk-based zoning.
- Long-term storage and conveyance investments.

## Energy (ECN-E)

### ECN-E1 Decarbonization

- Energy system aligned with greenhouse gas budget (ECN-B1).
- Phase-out trajectory for fossil fuels in legislation.
- Just-transition support for affected workers and communities (ECO-W3).

### ECN-E2 Resource Mix

- Renewable generation as backbone (solar, wind, hydro, geothermal where appropriate).
- Storage at multiple scales (battery, pumped hydro, hydrogen where feasible).
- Nuclear retained or expanded where local consent, safety, and waste-management conditions are met.
- Demand reduction through efficiency and design.

### ECN-E3 Grid and Resilience

- Grid modernization for high-renewable shares.
- Distributed generation supported.
- Redundancy: regional grids interconnected with islanding capability.
- Critical-infrastructure cyber-resilience (RSK-008).

### ECN-E4 Universal Access

- Basic energy as commons floor (ECO-L1).
- Affordability protected.
- Energy poverty addressed structurally.

### ECN-E5 Heat

- District heating where dense urbanism makes it efficient.
- Heat-pump conversion at scale for buildings.
- Industrial-heat decarbonization with sectoral roadmaps.

### ECN-E6 Transport Energy

- Electric and active mobility infrastructure prioritized; combustion phased out over defined trajectory.
- Aviation and shipping addressed through fuels-policy and demand-side measures.

## Food (ECN-F)

### ECN-F1 Food Sovereignty

- Domestic capacity to feed members from domestic and regional sources for staples.
- Strategic food reserves for emergency.
- Imports for variety and efficiency, not for basic survival.

### ECN-F2 Agricultural Systems

- Regenerative agriculture as default direction.
- Soil-quality protection; agro-ecology research and extension.
- Livestock systems reformed for animal welfare and ecological footprint.
- Fisheries managed within sustainable yield with adaptive management.

### ECN-F3 Food Access and Nutrition

- Right to food (LAW-R2.4).
- School-meal programs of nutritional standard.
- Food-insecurity measurement and response.
- Anti-food-waste programs.

### ECN-F4 Local Production Floors

- Each region maintains capacity to produce defined share of own staple food.
- Urban agriculture, community gardens, distributed production supported.

### ECN-F5 Animal Welfare

- Industrial confinement systems phased out.
- Welfare standards aligned with current scientific consensus.
- Transparency on practices.

## Materials and Circular Flows (ECN-M)

### ECN-M1 Extended Producer Responsibility

- Producers responsible for full life-cycle of products including end-of-life.
- Modular and repairable design favored through regulation and tax.
- Right to repair.

### ECN-M2 Circular Material Flows

- Recycling and remanufacturing infrastructure.
- Phase-out of unrecyclable single-use packaging.
- Material-passport requirements for major construction and product categories.

### ECN-M3 Hazardous Materials

- Strict regulation, with phase-out of unnecessary high-risk substances.
- Worker and community protection.
- International cooperation on transboundary movements.

## Transport and Mobility (ECN-T)

### ECN-T1 Hierarchy

- Walking, cycling, public transit first; shared mobility next; private motorized transport last.
- Urban design supports the hierarchy (ECN-L3).

### ECN-T2 Public Transit

- High-frequency, affordable, universal-access transit in urban areas.
- Regional rail and bus networks for inter-cell mobility.
- Accessibility for disabled members.

### ECN-T3 Logistics

- Freight optimized for emissions and resilience.
- Rail and waterway for long-haul; electric road for last-mile.
- Strategic-stock distribution capacity.

### ECN-T4 Rural and Remote

- Subsidized mobility for low-density areas.
- Community-mobility services where commercial provision is infeasible.

## Infrastructure Continuity (ECN-IC)

### ECN-IC1 Critical-Infrastructure Categories

- Energy (electricity, heat, fuel).
- Water and sanitation.
- Food and supply.
- Health.
- Communications.
- Transport.
- Financial services and payments.
- Public-safety services.

### ECN-IC2 Resilience Standards

- Defined recovery-time targets for each category.
- Redundancy requirements.
- Stockpile and reserve requirements.
- Cybersecurity baseline.
- Physical security baseline.
- Regular drills and after-action reviews.

### ECN-IC3 Public Ownership of Critical Networks

- Backbone grid, water systems, major transit, basic communications backbone are publicly owned or strongly regulated; private operators within strict regulatory regime where applicable.

### ECN-IC4 Decentralization Where Beneficial

- Energy: distributed generation in addition to centralized.
- Food: local floors in addition to broader markets.
- Communications: mesh and resilient overlays in addition to backbone.

## Climate Adaptation (ECN-CA)

### ECN-CA1 Risk-Based Planning

- National and regional climate-risk assessments updated every 5 years.
- Maps of heat, flood, fire, drought, sea-level, biodiversity, agricultural risk.

### ECN-CA2 Infrastructure Hardening

- Coastal protection or managed retreat.
- Stormwater capacity for heavier events.
- Wildfire-resilient design.
- Heat-resilient urban design and cooling centers.

### ECN-CA3 Migration and Settlement

- Internal climate-migration anticipation (RSK-012).
- Reception infrastructure in receiving regions.
- Sending-region transition support.

### ECN-CA4 Health and Climate

- Pairs with HEA-P4.
- Heat health plans, vector-disease surveillance, mental-health support post-disaster.

## Ecological Accounting (ECN-A)

### ECN-A1 National Accounts

- Material and energy throughput, emissions, biodiversity, water, soil tracked alongside GDP (ECO-MK1, CLM-120).
- Reports annual.

### ECN-A2 Project-Level Assessment

- Environmental Impact Assessment required for major projects.
- Strategic Environmental Assessment for plans and programs.
- Cumulative-impact assessment for sensitive regions.

### ECN-A3 Trade Accounting

- Embedded emissions and resource-use in imports tracked.
- Border carbon adjustment where consistent with international cooperation.
- Trade-policy aligned with ecological constraints.

## Indicators (ECN-I)

- Emissions trajectory vs. budget.
- Material footprint per capita.
- Biodiversity intactness.
- Water-stress and quality.
- Soil quality.
- Pollution levels by medium.
- Recovery times from disruptions.
- Reserve durations by sector.
- Housing affordability and quality distribution.
- Energy poverty rate.
- Food security rate.
- Active-transport mode share.
- Infrastructure-maintenance backlog.

## Open Issues

- Hard ecological caps (ECN-B1) interact with growth and employment expectations; transition and just-transition policy must do heavy work.
- Nuclear (ECN-E2) is politically contested; framework allows it subject to local consent and safety.
- Land-value taxation (ECN-L1, ECO-T3) is administratively demanding and politically contested; transition path required.
- Animal-welfare reforms (ECN-F5) have economic and cultural costs; phase-out timelines need negotiation.
- Cap-and-dividend (ECN-B3) versus standards-based regulation is a recurring policy choice; the framework allows both depending on sector.
- Geoengineering governance (RSK-041) is barely addressed; future iteration should specify.
- The framework assumes substantial behavior change is feasible (B-019); if not, ecological targets are at risk.
