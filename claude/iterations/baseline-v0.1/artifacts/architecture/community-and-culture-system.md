# Community and Culture System

Artifact ID: ART-014
Iteration: baseline-v0.1
Status: draft
Change type: new
Owner module: MOD-CUL
Depends on: ART-008, ART-011, ART-012

## Purpose

Specify the social architecture: minimum viable community, functional roles, inter-community relationships, isolation resilience, conflict resolution, ritual and meaning, care infrastructure, and pluralism. Bound by `value-constitution.md` (ART-008), `law-and-justice-system.md` (ART-011), and `health-and-mental-health-system.md` (ART-012).

The animating principles: belonging without coercion, pluralism without supremacy, care as infrastructure, and conflict-as-feature handled through standing repair mechanisms.

## Community Cell (CUL-C)

### CUL-C1 Minimum Viable Cell

- Working scale: 5,000-15,000 members; lower bound for institutional sufficiency, upper bound for relational legibility.
- Includes: housing, primary care, primary education, food retail, public space, transit access, community center, places of gathering across belief and lifestyle communities.
- Reflects B-007 (group size and governance form), Dunbar layering, and Ostrom-style local commons feasibility.

### CUL-C2 Functional Roles

Every cell should be able to staff (paid or voluntary) the following functions. Roles do not require single individuals; mixed and shared roles are common.

- Caregivers (children, elders, disabled members).
- Educators (formal and informal).
- Mediators (conflict resolution).
- Healers (clinical and non-clinical).
- Builders and makers (construction, repair, food, craft).
- Stewards (ecological, infrastructure).
- Connectors (welcoming, social integration).
- Witnesses (record-keepers, oral and written).
- Public-service personnel (governance, emergency, communication).

No role grants status superiority; rotating leadership of cell-level councils.

### CUL-C3 Cell-Level Governance

- Local Council (per GOV-T1), Local Manager, Local Ombuds, Local Conflict Forum.
- Cell budget and discretion within tier-2 envelope.
- Direct participation through neighborhood assemblies, deliberative bodies, sortition for specific questions.

### CUL-C4 Cell Observability

- Local-budget execution, infrastructure status, public-safety incident data, conflict-resolution outcomes, wellbeing indicators visible to members.
- Care for privacy: individual cases not exposed in aggregates.
- See VAL-019 and TZ-004.

## Inter-Community Relations (CUL-I)

### CUL-I1 Federation

- Cells federate at regional tier (GOV-T2); resource-sharing protocols specified.
- Cells may co-operate across regional boundaries on specific concerns (watershed, transport, cultural events).

### CUL-I2 Mobility and Exit

- Members may move between cells freely (LAW-R1 freedom of movement).
- Receiving cells provide reception services; integration support exists.
- Right of exit from any community or religious group preserved; coercion against exit prohibited.

### CUL-I3 Asymmetries and Disputes

- Inter-cell disputes (resource, environmental, conflict) escalate to regional tier with mediation; ultimately to courts (LAW-C).
- Cells with limited capacity receive supplemental support; no race-to-the-bottom on services.

### CUL-I4 Isolation Resilience

- Each cell can operate critical services for at least 30 days without supply from outside; key inputs (water, energy, food, medicine, communication) buffered.
- Regional-level resilience targets 6 to 24 months for severe disruption.
- See ART-015.

## Pluralism and Identity (CUL-P)

### CUL-P1 Equal Civic Membership

- Membership in the polity is open and equal; civic identity does not require ethnic, religious, or cultural homogeneity.
- Naturalization pathways accessible; long-term residents have substantial rights.

### CUL-P2 Cultural and Religious Communities

- Free formation of and participation in cultural, religious, philosophical, and lifestyle communities.
- Self-governance within communities is permitted to the extent it does not violate universal rights, individual exit, and child rights.
- Public funding for cultural infrastructure is non-discriminatory.

### CUL-P3 Anti-Discrimination

- Strong anti-discrimination law (LAW-R1).
- Discrimination audits in employment, housing, services.

### CUL-P4 Minority Rights

- Linguistic, religious, ethnic, indigenous minorities have protected rights to language, education, cultural institutions.
- Targeted public funding to support minority-cultural maintenance.

### CUL-P5 Civic Cohesion Without Forced Assimilation

- Shared civic language(s) for public business; multilingual public services where significant linguistic minorities exist.
- Shared civic rituals (holidays, civic ceremonies, public commemorations) inclusive of pluralism.
- Civic-education curriculum (EDU-C2) shapes shared knowledge without prescribing belief.

### CUL-P6 Newcomers, Dissenters, Minorities

- Reception infrastructure for immigrants and refugees.
- Protection for dissenters against social, employment, and informal sanctions beyond legal limits.
- Active inclusion strategies for marginalized groups.

## Care Infrastructure (CUL-CA)

### CUL-CA1 Universal Care

- Child care, elder care, disability care, family support, caregiver respite — all available at cell or regional level.
- See VAL-017 and ECO-W.

### CUL-CA2 Family Diversity

- Recognition of diverse family forms (nuclear, extended, chosen, single-parent, multi-generational, kinship, multi-parent within legal limits).
- Family-support policies do not privilege a single form.

### CUL-CA3 Intergenerational Connection

- Housing and public-space design supports inter-generational mixing (ART-015).
- Programs that connect children, working-age adults, and elders.
- Intergenerational stewardship framing in education and civic life.

### CUL-CA4 Special Populations

- Foster care and out-of-home care under strict rights protections for children.
- Disability community: accessibility, accommodation, peer support, exit from coercive institutions.
- Refugees and asylum seekers integrated under ART-017.

### CUL-CA5 Loneliness Prevention

- Anti-isolation infrastructure (HEA-M5) embedded in zoning and community design.
- Programs for at-risk populations (elders living alone, new parents, recently bereaved, post-discharge patients, new arrivals).
- See CLM-042.

## Conflict Resolution (CUL-CR)

### CUL-CR1 Layered Approach

- Layer 1: informal mediation between parties.
- Layer 2: community-based restorative conferencing.
- Layer 3: ombuds-facilitated mediation.
- Layer 4: civil or administrative tribunal.
- Layer 5: court adjudication.

Use of higher layers requires either failure of lower layers, severity of the conflict, or party preference. Coercion to use lower layers is prohibited.

### CUL-CR2 Capacity

- Trained mediators in every cell.
- Restorative-justice programs networked with criminal justice (LAW-CJ3, CLM-091).
- Family-conflict mediation as default in family disputes where rights are not at stake.
- Workplace mediation services available.

### CUL-CR3 Cultural Practices

- Customary and community conflict-resolution practices respected within constitutional limits (LAW-S5).
- Where customary process violates rights or coerces parties, parties retain access to formal mechanisms.

## Ritual, Meaning, and Civic Life (CUL-R)

### CUL-R1 Civic Rituals

- Constitution Day, naturalization ceremonies, civic-service recognitions, election rituals, memorials.
- Shared without compulsion; participation voluntary.

### CUL-R2 Religious and Spiritual Life

- Religious holidays and observances respected; reasonable workplace accommodation; multi-faith holiday calendars in education.
- Public spaces for religious and spiritual gathering protected.

### CUL-R3 Cultural Production

- Public arts funding (proportional small share of budget) for community arts, public commissions, support of artists.
- Open access to cultural heritage; libraries, museums, archives.
- Protection of indigenous cultural heritage and intellectual property.

### CUL-R4 Grief and Memory

- Public mourning rituals for major losses (pandemic, war, disaster).
- Memorialization processes with community participation.
- Truth-and-reconciliation infrastructure where historical harms require it (see ART-017 for migration / refugee context; ART-011 for criminal justice integration).

### CUL-R5 Celebration

- Festivals, harvest rituals, life-cycle celebrations, sport, music, food traditions supported in public space.

## Media and Information (CUL-MI)

### CUL-MI1 Public-Interest Media

- Public-service broadcasting with independent funding (constitutional floor); arms-length governance.
- Investigative journalism support (grants, training, legal-defense fund).
- Local-news infrastructure protected against collapse.

### CUL-MI2 Platform Accountability

- Major social platforms regulated for transparency, algorithmic accountability, child safety, and election integrity.
- See ART-016.

### CUL-MI3 Information Literacy

- Civic and media literacy required in education (EDU-C1).
- Library and public-knowledge infrastructure funded.

### CUL-MI4 Speech and Harm

- Strong speech protection per LAW-R1.
- Narrow exceptions: defamation, threats, incitement, exploitation, hate speech tied to imminent harm or systematic discrimination — defined narrowly and reviewed judicially.
- Anonymous speech protected for public-interest purposes; abuse of anonymity for harassment subject to discovery procedures with judicial oversight.

## Demographic and Family Policy (CUL-DF)

### CUL-DF1 Family Support

- Paid parental leave (gender-equal where possible) of substantial length.
- Universal child benefit.
- Child-care access (EDU-P1).
- Family-friendly working arrangements.

### CUL-DF2 Demographic Concerns

- The framework neither targets a fertility rate nor coerces family formation.
- Care infrastructure and economic security support family choices without compulsion (RSK-013).

### CUL-DF3 Migration and Integration

- Coordinated with ART-017; reception infrastructure detailed there.
- Integration is two-way: new arrivals adapt to civic norms (constitutional, legal); receiving communities adapt to plural community composition.

## Indicators (CUL-I)

- Trust: generalized social trust; trust in institutions.
- Connection: loneliness rate; participation in associations and clubs; community-event attendance.
- Conflict: incidence and resolution rates by mechanism; rates of escalation to courts.
- Inclusion: discrimination-audit findings; minority-rights compliance; exit rates from communities.
- Family wellbeing: family time, caregiving burden distribution, child wellbeing index.
- Cultural participation: arts, sport, religious, civic.
- Local resilience: cell-level reserve durations; preparedness drills.
- Media: news consumption, source diversity, exposure to misinformation.

## Open Issues

- Cell scale (CUL-C1) is a heuristic; actual cell scale should reflect local geography, density, and identity boundaries. Pilot data needed.
- "Right of exit" enforcement against high-coercion groups (CUL-P2) is difficult in practice; the framework relies on universal rights, child protection, and shelter access.
- Speech-and-harm boundary (CUL-MI4) will need recalibration as platform architecture evolves; framework leans toward narrow harm tests.
- Indigenous and customary-law accommodation (CUL-CR3) is sensitive; the framework defaults to participatory design with affected communities.
- Demographic policy (CUL-DF2) is morally contested; the framework declines to set population targets.
- Civic-ritual design (CUL-R1) risks majoritarianism; pluralist participation is required by design.
