# MOD-DEF: Defense and External Relations

## Purpose

Define how the society survives external threats while preserving its values, legitimacy, and internal freedoms.

## Design Requirements

- Civil defense and disaster readiness.
- Deterrence model and military doctrine consistent with the value constitution.
- Alliance strategy, diplomacy, trade policy, and sanctions policy.
- Migration, asylum, refugee integration, and border governance.
- Supply-chain security and strategic reserves.
- Protection against information warfare, sabotage, coups, and institutional capture attempts.
- Explicit external boundary design for physical access, cyber access, data exchange, trade dependencies, migration channels, biosecurity, and sensitive knowledge transfer.
- Emergency powers with clear limits, review, expiration, and remedy.

## Core Questions

- RQ-DEF-001: What threats must the society be able to deter or survive?
- RQ-DEF-002: How can defense readiness avoid militarizing civil society?
- RQ-DEF-003: What external dependencies are acceptable, and which are existential risks?
- RQ-DEF-004: How are migrants and refugees integrated consistently with rights and resilience?
- RQ-DEF-005: What crisis powers are necessary, and how are they constrained?
- RQ-DEF-006: Which external boundaries are required for security, and how are they audited without becoming isolationist or abusive?
